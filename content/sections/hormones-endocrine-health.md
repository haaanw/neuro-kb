# Hormones & Endocrine Health

> Canonical synthesis from 36 ingested episodes (multi-creator). Confidence-graded; creator claims flagged where they outrun evidence.
> Sources are science communicators (Huberman Lab, Peter Attia/The Drive, Rhonda Patrick/FoundMyFitness, Robert Sapolsky/Stanford). Verify quantitative and protocol claims against primary literature before clinical use.

## Overview

This section covers the endocrine systems most relevant to brain function, behavior, performance, and longevity: the sex steroids (testosterone, estradiol, progesterone, DHEA, DHT), the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis, the thyroid axis, growth hormone / IGF-1, the stress axis (cortisol/CRH), prolactin, and the peptide therapies that act on these systems. It spans the full lifespan — sexual differentiation in utero, puberty, fertility, perimenopause/menopause and andropause — and integrates both the mechanistic neuroendocrinology (Sapolsky, Shah, Breedlove) and the practical optimization framing (Gillett, Attia, Huberman, Crawford, Haver).

The core, well-established science is consistent across creators: hormones are not single "levels" but ratios and rhythms operating through feedback loops. Most circulating steroid is protein-bound (chiefly to SHBG), so total, free, and bioavailable fractions tell different stories. Sex differences arise developmentally from a cascade triggered by the SRY gene and a perinatal androgen "organizational" window, then are "activated" by circulating hormones later in life — the organizational/activational distinction is one of the most durable findings in behavioral endocrinology. The menstrual cycle is a tightly choreographed feedback system (rising estradiol must stay high enough, long enough — roughly 200 pg/mL for ~48-50 hours — to trigger the LH surge and ovulation).

Where the science is genuinely emerging or contested: the optimal use of hormone replacement (TRT in eugonadal men, MHT/HRT in menopause), the risk-benefit of growth-hormone-axis peptides, the magnitude of endocrine-disruptor effects on human fertility, and the boundary between treating deficiency versus enhancing already-normal physiology. Several creators (notably the more optimization-oriented guests) extend plausible mechanisms into confident protocols that the evidence does not fully support.

A recurring, defensible theme: lifestyle interventions (resistance training, appropriate caloric/metabolic management, sleep, stress regulation, light exposure) are the highest-leverage, lowest-risk levers for hormone health, and they should precede pharmacology. Metabolic health (insulin sensitivity, body composition) is upstream of much sex-hormone dysregulation — most clearly in PCOS and in obesity-associated low testosterone. The biggest practical caveat throughout: exogenous hormones and peptides carry real, dose-dependent risks (sleep apnea, fertility suppression, cancer-growth promotion) that the enthusiast framing often understates.

## Key findings by theme

### 1. How hormones actually work: ratios, binding, and rhythms
- Most circulating sex steroid is bound to SHBG; "free" testosterone is the smallest fraction, and binding affinity ranks DHT > testosterone > androstenedione/DHEA > estradiol. Measuring only free T misses the picture. **[high]**
- Women have substantially more total testosterone than estradiol (estradiol is measured in pg/mL, testosterone in ng/dL) and even more DHEA — testosterone is a major, under-appreciated female hormone, not a trace one. **[high]**
- Hormone effects depend on receptor sensitivity, not just blood level — the androgen receptor gene is on the X chromosome (men inherit it maternally), and receptor density/sensitivity varies by genetics and lifestyle. This is why "the same testosterone level feels different for every man" (Hooven). **[high]**
- Dopamine and prolactin operate as an antagonistic pair, as do estrogen/progesterone and testosterone/estradiol — health is about balance and rhythm, not maximizing any single hormone (Gillett's "dopamine wave pool"). **[medium — useful framing, mechanistically grounded but simplified]**
- Prolactin inhibits gonadotropin release (lowering testosterone); estrogen upregulates the PRL gene, raising prolactin. **[high]**

### 2. Sexual differentiation, sex differences & orientation (developmental neuroendocrinology)
- Embryos are anatomically indistinguishable for ~5-6 weeks; the SRY gene initiates testicular development, and a fetal testosterone surge (~peaking 15-20 weeks gestation) organizes male-typical development. Male fetal T runs roughly 10-20x female levels during this window. **[high]**
- Organizational vs. activational effects: perinatal hormone exposure permanently "wires" neural circuits (organizational); later circulating hormones "activate" behaviors via that wiring. This is a foundational, replicated principle (Breedlove, Shah, Sapolsky). **[high]**
- Animal models show hormonally-mediated behavioral circuits can be dramatically altered — e.g., manipulating specific neurons changed the male mouse sexual refractory period from days to ~1 second — but extrapolation to subjective human experience is explicitly cautioned against. **[high for the animal finding; low for human inference]**
- Sexual orientation has measurable biological correlates (e.g., the "gay ram" not mounting available females; prenatal hormone markers), consistent with prenatal hormonal contributions — but orientation is multifactorial and not reducible to a single hormone (Breedlove, Sapolsky). **[medium]**
- Testosterone's link to "aggression": T does not create aggression de novo; it amplifies pre-existing tendencies and is more about social challenge/status and effort than raw violence (Sapolsky). **[high]**

### 3. Male hormone health, testosterone & TRT
- Resistance training (heavy, ~1RM up to 6-8 rep range) acutely raises testosterone for roughly a day, sometimes up to 48h. Endurance exercise beyond ~75 minutes tends to lower testosterone, likely via cortisol. **[medium — direction is well-supported; magnitude/duration vary]**
- In obese/metabolic-syndrome men, caloric restriction and fat loss raise testosterone. In young, lean, healthy men, caloric restriction can *lower* testosterone (Mayo Clinic Proceedings systematic review cited). Context determines direction. **[high]**
- TRT does not cause prostate cancer, but testosterone will grow an existing prostate cancer; autopsy prevalence of prostate cancer is ~50% by age 80 and ~90% by 90-100, so this is a real consideration in aging men. **[high]**
- TRT (and exogenous androgens generally) raises sleep-apnea risk in a dose-dependent way, even in men who were eugonadal before starting. Early TRT can also induce a transient hyper-sympathetic state and disrupted sleep. **[high]**
- Smoked marijuana lowers testosterone primarily by increasing aromatase (raising estradiol, which suppresses LH/FSH); cannabinoids (THC/CBD) themselves don't directly lower T. High alcohol and potent GABA agonists (barbiturates, benzodiazepines) lower testosterone. Opioids strongly suppress LH/FSH and testosterone. **[medium-high]**
- DHT is a strong androgen (drives libido, drive, effort-feels-good, but also male-pattern baldness and prostate growth). Plant polyphenols (e.g., curcumin/turmeric + black pepper extract) can inhibit 5-alpha-reductase and lower DHT. For hair loss without systemic anti-androgen side effects, *localized* dutasteride mesotherapy is presented as the most promising approach. **[medium — mechanism sound; mesotherapy evidence base is thin]**

### 4. Female hormone health, the menstrual cycle, PCOS & fertility
- Ovulation requires estradiol to reach a threshold and sustain it (~200 pg/mL for ~48-50h), triggering the LH surge; the ruptured follicle becomes the corpus luteum, which produces progesterone and lives ~2 weeks absent pregnancy. A luteal phase under ~11 days is "short" but can still produce regular cycles. **[high]**
- PCOS is under-diagnosed (prevalence estimates ~10-20%), diagnosed by Rotterdam criteria (androgen excess, ovulatory dysfunction, polycystic ovaries — only 2 of 3 required; ultrasound not mandatory). Signs: hirsutism, androgenic acne, oligomenorrhea (>35-day cycles or <9 periods/year), insulin resistance (HOMA-IR >2, fasting insulin >6), infertility. **[high]**
- Insulin resistance is frequently upstream of PCOS; insulin sensitization (metformin, myo-inositol; D-chiro-inositol acts as a weak anti-androgen) and body-composition improvement are first-line tools. Not everyone with PCOS needs metformin. **[high]**
- Fertility-workup thresholds: under 35, evaluate after 12 months trying; 35+, after 6 months. **[high]**
- Endometriosis is commonly missed — recurrent "UTI"/bladder symptoms with negative cultures and repeated antibiotic courses should raise suspicion ("endometriosis until proven otherwise," Aliabadi). **[medium — clinically reasonable heuristic, not a formal guideline]**
- Female testosterone matters for vitality/health optimization; estrogen and progesterone matter more for pathology prevention (bone, breast). **[high]**

### 5. Menopause, perimenopause & hormone therapy
- Vasomotor symptoms (hot flashes/night sweats) of menopause — and the analogous "andropause" in men — are a major driver of disrupted sleep, mediated by declining progestogen/sex-steroid activity. **[high]**
- Resistance/strength training is repeatedly emphasized as essential for women in menopause (bone, muscle, metabolic health) and is one of the few uncontested, high-value interventions across this life stage (Lyon, Boyle, Cavaliere; Haver; Attia). **[high]**
- Menopausal hormone therapy (MHT/HRT) is presented as substantially safer and more beneficial for many women than the post-WHI era assumed — but optimal timing ("window"), formulation, and individual risk stratification remain genuinely debated. **[medium — directionally supported by re-analysis of WHI, but creators sometimes overstate consensus]**

### 6. Thyroid & metabolism
- T4 has a half-life of ~8 days; T3 ~12 hours. T4 is the storage/pro-hormone, T3 the active form — tissue-level T3 activation (deiodinases) can diverge from blood levels (e.g., cold exposure can spike T3 locally in brown fat without changing blood T3). **[high]**
- Untreated hypothyroidism carries serious mortality consequences (a cited ~5x mortality difference if thyroid replacement is withheld where indicated). Diagnosis and adequate replacement matter (Bianco, Attia #373). **[high — directionally; exact figure context-dependent]**
- Some patients on T4-only replacement remain symptomatic and may benefit from T3-containing approaches — an area of active change in practice. **[medium]**
- Growth hormone surges overnight and is enhanced by fasting; avoiding food 2-3h before sleep preserves much of the nighttime GH pulse (law of diminishing returns beyond that). Exogenous IGF-1 at physiologic doses does not improve body composition — the body-comp benefit of exercise comes from *local* (autocrine/paracrine) IGF-1, not endocrine IGF-1. **[medium-high]**

### 7. Stress, cortisol & the psychology of stress hormones
- Predictability and control modulate the stress response, but only within a narrow temporal window: a warning ~10s before a stressor reduces stress; a warning 2 minutes before makes it worse (dread). Predictive information only helps in a narrow domain (Sapolsky, "grinding wheel" study). **[high]**
- Stress-management efficacy is individual: a technique only works if you'll actually do it consistently and it doesn't itself stress you. Deciding your well-being is a daily priority and reliably spending ~20-30 min/day on *some* practice gets you "~80% of the way there." **[high for principle; the 80% figure is illustrative, low precision]**
- Chronic stress/cortisol interacts with sex-steroid and thyroid axes (e.g., prolonged endurance/cortisol suppressing testosterone). **[high]**

### 8. Peptides & hormone therapies (high-interest, high-caution)
- Peptides are heterogeneous — ranging from life-saving (insulin) to high-risk. Insulin and growth hormone are themselves peptide hormones; dosing errors can be fatal. Physician supervision and clean (LPS-free) compounding-pharmacy sourcing are stressed; non-prescription sources often carry endotoxin (LPS) contamination causing inflammation/fever mistaken for "it working." **[high on the safety/sourcing point]**
- GH-axis peptides (GHRPs/GHRH analogs) carry tumor-growth/cancer risk (high-GH states associate with elevated cancer incidence); most claimed benefits (lipolysis, lean mass, cosmetic) are achievable by safer means — so most people don't need them. **[medium-high]**
- BPC-157: increases VEGF/angiogenesis (rationale for tissue repair); for that same reason it is contraindicated in cancer or high cancer risk (opposite mechanism to anti-VEGF cancer drugs like Avastin). Not FDA-approved; anecdotally tolerated short-term. **[low-medium — popular, mechanistically plausible, minimal human RCT evidence; healing claims are anecdotal]**
- Melanotan/PT-141 family: bremelanotide (PT-141) is FDA-approved for hypoactive sexual desire disorder (acts centrally); melanocortin agonists are also approved for lipodystrophy and a rare obesity-linked genetic condition. Caution with melanoma/family history (alpha-MSH pathway). **[high on approvals; medium on broader use]**

## Actionable protocols

Consolidated and deduplicated from the extracted protocols. Dosages and timings are creator recommendations — verify before clinical use.

**Foundational lifestyle (Gillett's "big six"): diet, exercise, stress, sleep, sunlight/outdoors, spirit**
- Prioritize a small amount of consistent intervention over large inconsistent efforts ("law of diminishing returns"). Diet and resistance training are the two strongest levers.
- Get **150-180 min/week minimum of zone 2 cardio** (Gillett). The more zone 2 you do, the less you need prolonged caloric restriction.
- Resistance training for hormone optimization: work in the **~1RM to 6-8 rep range** (raises testosterone for ~24-48h in both sexes). Keep endurance bouts under ~75 min to avoid cortisol-driven T suppression.
- A sample strength/hormone session (French): **80% 1RM, ~6 sets of 10 reps, ~2 min rest**.
- Caloric restriction / intermittent fasting: beneficial if obese/metabolic syndrome (raises T); for lean healthy people, only do it at caloric maintenance to avoid lowering T. Caveat: young lean men who under-eat can suppress testosterone.

**Diagnostics**
- Get blood work **every 3-6 months** for prevention; test both fasted and non-fasted. Advocate for hormone panels by reporting functional symptoms (energy, focus, performance, libido) — pathology is not required to justify testing.

**Growth hormone / sleep**
- To preserve the overnight GH pulse, **avoid food in the 2-3 h before sleep**; fasting enhances GH (diminishing returns the longer you go).

**Female fertility / cycle**
- Track for the LH-surge prerequisite: estradiol sustained high (~200 pg/mL for ~48-50h). Evaluate fertility after 12 months trying (<35) or 6 months (35+).
- PCOS with insulin-resistance phenotype: prioritize body-composition/metabolic treatment; consider myo-inositol (insulin sensitizer) ± metformin; D-chiro-inositol for anti-androgen effect.

**Sexual health**
- Lube/arousal timing: consider applying lubricant ~30 min before intercourse; extended foreplay (>~21 min) strongly associates with female orgasm. Build deliberate intimacy (~20 min, a few times/week) to support desire (Attia #371).
- Post-C-section: expect ~6 weeks for healing absent complications before resuming activity.

**Hair loss without systemic anti-androgen effects**
- Consider physician-directed **localized dutasteride mesotherapy** (scalp-only 5-AR inhibition) rather than systemic finasteride/dutasteride, to spare libido/drive/prostate. (Evidence base is limited — discuss with a physician.)

**Stress management**
- Pick a practice you'll actually do **~20-30 min daily or every other day**; consistency and personal fit matter more than the specific technique. Don't reserve it only for acute moments.

**Peptides (only with a physician)**
- Source exclusively from reputable compounding pharmacies that remove LPS. Treat "tingling/mild fever" on injection as a red flag for endotoxin, not efficacy. Avoid BPC-157 if you have or are at risk for cancer. Avoid GH-axis peptides unless there's a clear indication. Avoid melanocortin agonists with melanoma history.

**Substances**
- Minimize smoked marijuana (raises aromatase → estradiol → suppresses T), high alcohol, and unnecessary opioids/strong GABA agonists if testosterone/fertility is a concern. Caffeine is hormonally near-neutral *unless* it disrupts sleep.

## Contested / where creator framing outruns evidence

- **"Optimization" of already-normal hormones via TRT/peptides.** The line between treating deficiency and enhancing normal physiology is repeatedly blurred. TRT in eugonadal men measurably raises sleep-apnea risk and suppresses fertility; framing it as broadly low-risk lifestyle optimization understates this. **[creator opinion > evidence]**
- **BPC-157 healing claims.** Presented as "essentially standard of care" with dramatic anecdotes (e.g., a gymnast's Achilles healing in a month). Human RCT evidence is minimal; this is mechanism + anecdote, not established efficacy. **[low confidence; treat as speculative]**
- **The "big six pillars," especially "spirit."** A reasonable holistic framing, but the claim that spiritual health affects the body "physiologically" is asserted, not quantified — opinion, not measured endocrinology.
- **Dutasteride mesotherapy** as "most promising" for hair loss: plausible but with a thin evidence base relative to the confidence expressed.
- **Menopausal hormone therapy consensus.** The shift toward viewing MHT as safe/beneficial is directionally supported by WHI re-analysis, but some episodes present it as more settled than the literature (timing window, formulation, individual risk) warrants.
- **Precise quantitative claims** (e.g., "5x mortality," "4x results," "80% of the way there," "10x effect size") are frequently illustrative or context-dependent rather than rigorously transferable — verify against the primary studies.
- **Single-hormone determinism for complex behavior** (aggression, sexual orientation): Sapolsky and Breedlove explicitly push back on this; where lighter episodes imply tidy causation, the careful neuroendocrinology says permissive/probabilistic, not deterministic.
- **Endocrine disruptors and human fertility (Swan):** the mechanistic concern (phthalates/EDCs) is real and the animal data are strong, but the magnitude of population-level human fertility effects and attribution remain debated.

## Deep Research audit — Testosterone boosters & TRT cardiovascular/fertility safety (DR-006)

> Autonomous web deep-research (agent), cited to primary sources. Full report: [[DR-006]]. Note: [[DR-006-audit]]

**Executive summary:** The best RCT-grade lifestyle lever on endogenous testosterone is weight loss in overweight/obese men: ~10% diet-induced loss raises total T ~2-3 nmol/L (~85 ng/dL); bariatric surgery (~30% loss) up to ~8.7 nmol/L (~250 ng/dL). Sleep restriction may lower T ~10-15% (one crossover) but a later RCT found no change — contested. Exercise has negligible effect on resting T in meta-analysis. Among supplements, marketing outruns evidence: ashwagandha has the most RCT support (~+90-120 ng/dL in small, often industry-funded trials) but a real, RUCAM-confirmed hepatotoxicity signal (one transplant; 2024 TGA alert). Tongkat Ali shows modest benefit in older/low-T men. Zinc helps mainly by correcting deficiency. Boron is thin/inconsistent in men. Fadogia agrestis has zero human trials and rodent testicular-toxicity data — pure creator/podcast hype. On TRT: TRAVERSE (NEJM 2023, n=5,246 high-CV-risk hypogonadal men, transdermal gel) showed MACE noninferiority (HR 0.96, 95% CI 0.78-1.17) but higher atrial fibrillation, pulmonary embolism, and acute kidney injury. TRT predictably suppresses spermatogenesis (contraceptive effect, mostly reversible 6-12 months; hCG can preserve fertility) and raises hematocrit (keep <54%).

**Key findings:**
- **[high]** Weight loss reverses obesity-associated low testosterone dose-dependently: ~10% diet-induced loss raises total T ~2.9 nmol/L (~84 ng/dL); ~32% surgical loss up to ~8.7 nmol/L (~251 ng/dL). Strongest lifestyle lever. — <https://academic.oup.com/ejendo/article-abstract/168/6/829/6659681>
- **[high]** Exercise training has a negligible effect on resting/basal total and free testosterone in meta-analysis; transient post-workout spikes do not durably raise baseline. — <https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6339914/>
- **[medium]** Short sleep may lower daytime testosterone ~10-15% (1-week 5h crossover), but a later randomized study found no significant change — magnitude is contested. — <https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4445839/>
- **[medium]** Ashwagandha modestly raises testosterone in small RCTs (e.g., +96 vs +18 ng/dL with 600 mg/day x8 weeks) but trials are small, heterogeneous, and often industry-funded. — <https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/hsr2.741>
- **[medium]** Ashwagandha carries a real hepatotoxicity signal: multiple RUCAM 'probable' herb-induced liver injury cases (mixed/cholestatic/hepatocellular), one liver transplant, and a Feb 2024 Australian TGA safety alert (12 reports, 4 hospitalizations). — <https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10459262/>
- **[low]** Fadogia agrestis has no human clinical trials; claims rest on rodent studies that also show dose-dependent testicular toxicity. Popular (podcast/creator) framing far outruns evidence. — <https://www.droracle.ai/articles/164019/is-there-evidence-linking-fadogia-agrestis-to-increased-testosterone>
- **[high]** TRAVERSE (n=5,246 high-CV-risk hypogonadal men, transdermal testosterone gel) showed MACE noninferiority to placebo (HR 0.96, 95% CI 0.78-1.17) but higher atrial fibrillation, pulmonary embolism, and acute kidney injury in the testosterone group. — <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37326322/>
- **[high]** Exogenous testosterone suppresses LH/FSH and spermatogenesis, acting as a male contraceptive (azoospermia common); recovery in ~64-84% of men, median ~110 days, mostly within 6-12 months; hCG can preserve fertility. — <https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6305868/>
- **[high]** TRT raises hematocrit/erythrocytosis; guidelines advise monitoring every 3-6 months in year one then annually and keeping Hct <54%, though registry data suggest modest elevations may not clearly increase thromboembolic events. — <https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12670475/>

**Conflicts / uncertainty:** Sleep: the famous ~10-15% testosterone drop (UChicago 1-week 5h crossover) is contradicted by a later randomized study showing no significant change — reproducibility uncertain. TRAVERSE AF/PE/AKI signals were statistically present but on secondary/underpowered endpoints, in an old, ~70% diabetic, COVID-era cohort, and a prior 106-RCT meta-analysis found AF risk non-significant (MH-OR 1.44, 0.46-4.46) — causality unresolved. Hematocrit <54% is a precautionary guideline threshold not firmly outcome-validated (HEAT registry suggests modest elevations may not raise clot events). Ashwagandha shows benefit in small industry-funded RCTs while harm comes from uncontrolled case reports (confounded by supplement quality/adulteration) — both true incidence figures uncertain. Zinc benefit appears limited to correcting deficiency, with a null trial in replete patients.

**Do not absorb yet:**
- 'Fadogia agrestis is a proven natural testosterone booster' — no human trials; rodent data show testicular toxicity; creator/podcast hype only.
- 'Tongkat Ali + Fadogia stacks reliably raise T in healthy young men' — Tongkat Ali RCTs are in older/low-T men with modest effects; no data support stacks or healthy-young-male efficacy.
- 'Boron meaningfully raises testosterone in men' — a male RCT found no effect; low-confidence and inconsistent.
- 'Zinc boosts testosterone in everyone' — benefit appears confined to deficiency correction, not replete men.
