# Live Q&A & AMA (mixed topics)

> Canonical synthesis from 38 ingested episodes (multi-creator). Confidence-graded; creator claims flagged where they outrun evidence.

## Overview

This section aggregates 38 question-and-answer episodes (~260,000 words) spanning three science communicators — Andrew Huberman (Huberman Lab AMAs + live-event Q&As), Peter Attia (The Drive AMA sneak peeks), and Rhonda Patrick (FoundMyFitness Q&As, including two COVID-19 episodes). Because the format is audience-driven, the content is intentionally broad: sleep architecture, circadian light timing, caffeine, cold/heat exposure, exercise programming, supplements (longevity compounds, adaptogens, collagen/protein, creatine), vision/eye health, learning and focus, motivation and dopamine, and a handful of timely topics (COVID-19, fluoride, kratom). The unifying thread is *translation* — taking mechanistic findings and converting them into behavioral "tools."

The strongest, most repeated material is circadian and sleep physiology. The core model Huberman returns to in nearly every Q&A is a three-period 24-hour cycle: a morning anchor (bright light to the eyes, temperature rise, optional caffeine delay), a midday/afternoon maintenance window, and an evening wind-down (dim light, cooling, avoiding late caffeine/alcohol). The light-timing claims (morning bright-light viewing advances and stabilizes the clock; nighttime light suppresses melatonin and disrupts sleep) rest on a large, genuinely established literature. The "temperature minimum" framework for jet-lag and phase-shifting is also grounded in real circadian science, though the precise 2-hour-before-wake heuristic is a simplification.

Confidence drops sharply for the supplement and quantitative-dosing content. Many specific dosages (ashwagandha 2×300 mg, collagen 5–30 g, magnesium threonate ~145 mg, theanine 100–400 mg, NMN 1–2 g sublingual) are presented with apparent precision but reflect a mix of small human trials, mechanistic reasoning, and personal regimen. NMN/NR/resveratrol longevity claims in particular outrun the human evidence; Attia is notably more skeptical of these than Huberman. The COVID-19 episodes (early-pandemic) include hydroxychloroquine and vitamin-D discussion that should be read as time-stamped to 2020 knowledge, not current consensus.

Across all three creators the epistemics are reasonable for the genre — mechanisms are usually separated from speculation, and Attia in particular is explicit about evidence tiers (e.g., critiquing fiber epidemiology and longevity-drug hype). The main risk for a reader is treating the confident *numbers* as settled when many are dose ranges extrapolated from limited data or self-experimentation. Verify any quantitative or supplement claim against primary literature before acting on it.

## Key findings by theme

### Circadian light & morning routine
- Viewing bright light (ideally sunlight) within ~30–60 min of waking is the most powerful stimulus for daytime alertness and for setting the timer to fall asleep ~16 h later, via melanopsin retinal ganglion cells → suprachiasmatic nucleus → cortisol pulse. **[high]**
- Light through a windshield/window is far less effective; needs unfiltered outdoor light. Cloudy days require more time (clear ~5 min, light cloud ~10 min, heavy overcast ~20–30 min). **[medium]** (durations are practical heuristics, not trial-derived thresholds)
- Bright artificial indoor/phone light is generally too dim to substitute for morning sun, yet bright enough at night to disrupt the clock — an asymmetry in retinal sensitivity. **[high]** (qualitatively established; "too dim" depends on lux)
- Getting late-afternoon/evening low-solar-angle sunlight "inoculates" against the disruptive effect of nighttime artificial light and anchors the evening clock. **[medium]**
- In winter/short days, a ~900-lux LED panel or drawing tablet 90 min after waking can partially substitute and "trick" the melatonin system. **[low/medium]** (mechanism plausible; specific product/lux is creator practice)

### Sleep architecture & wind-down
- Avoid bright artificial light (especially overhead) between ~10 p.m. and 4 a.m.; dim screens; use red light if you must be awake mid-cycle. **[high]** for direction; **[medium]** for exact hours
- Evening heat (sauna/hot bath ≤20–30 min) triggers a compensatory ~1–3 °C core-temperature drop that aids sleep onset; keep the sleeping room cool. **[medium/high]**
- Caffeine within ~8–12 h of bedtime degrades slow-wave and REM sleep even if you "fall asleep fine"; cap afternoon caffeine (e.g., <100 mg after 4 p.m.). **[medium]** (caffeine half-life and late-day sleep disruption are well supported; 8–12 h is conservative)
- Alcohol and THC/CBD within ~8 h of bed degrade sleep architecture even when they aid sleep onset. **[high]** for alcohol; **[medium]** for cannabinoids
- Keep wake times consistent (±1 h) across weekends; prefer a short afternoon nap over sleeping in. Naps fine if not so long/late they impair night sleep. **[high]**
- Avoid large meals ~2 h before bed to support slow-wave sleep and overnight growth-hormone release. **[medium]**
- Sleep "stack": magnesium L-threonate (~145 mg), apigenin (~50 mg), theanine (100–400 mg), 30–60 min pre-bed; individual response varies (some get GI upset from Mg, vivid/disruptive dreams from theanine). **[low/medium]** (small evidence base; framed as optional/personalized)
- Melatonin supplements are usually supraphysiological doses and interact with other hormone systems; occasional use (jet lag) likely fine, chronic use — especially in children — discouraged. **[medium]**

### Temperature minimum & jet lag
- Temperature minimum ≈ 2 h before habitual wake time; it is the pivot for phase-shifting. **[medium]** (real circadian marker; 2-h rule is a simplification of individual variation)
- Light/exercise/caffeine in the hours *before* temp-min delays the clock (later sleep/wake); the same *after* temp-min advances it (earlier). Use deliberately to pre-adapt before travel. **[medium/high]**

### Caffeine
- Delay first caffeine ~90–120 min after waking to blunt the afternoon adenosine crash. **[low/medium]** (mechanistically reasoned; limited direct trial support)
- Typical tolerated doses 100–300 mg; strong coffee/espresso can reach 400–500 mg. **[medium]**
- A ~4-day to 1-week abstinence restores caffeine's cognitive/performance effect by resetting tolerance. **[medium]**

### Cold & heat exposure
- Brief cold (cold shower/immersion 1–3 min, generally ≤5–6 min) drives a surge of adrenaline/noradrenaline and a delayed thermogenic rebound that warms the body after exit — useful as a morning wake-up, not as evening cooling. **[medium]**
- Cold should be "uncomfortable but safe"; build duration gradually — do not start at 10 min. Don't stay chilled afterward beyond ~10–30 min. **[medium]** (safety guidance is sound)
- Sauna: studied protocols ~20 min/session at ~79 °C; associated in cohort data with cardiovascular/longevity benefit. **[medium]** (association from observational Finnish cohorts)

### Exercise & cardiorespiratory fitness
- ~150–200 min/week of "zone 2" cardio is the broadly accepted target (Attia-derived framing). **[high]** for general activity guideline; **[medium]** for the specific zone-2 emphasis
- VO2/intensity can be framed in METs (1 MET = 3.5 mL O2/kg/min). **[high]**
- A practical week: ~60–75 min sessions max; HIIT example (Airdyne 20 s hard / 10 s rest × ~8); take a full rest day after 2 consecutive resistance days. **[medium]** (sensible programming; personal protocol, not prescriptive)

### Supplements & longevity compounds
- Collagen 5–30 g/day (most studies 15–30 g) over ≥2 weeks modestly improves skin elasticity/appearance. **[medium]**
- Protein target ~1 g/lb body weight/day is a common heuristic; Huberman self-reports ~175–210 g/day. **[medium]** (target is widely cited; "per pound" is generous vs. some guidelines)
- Adaptogens (ashwagandha 2×250–300 mg/day) meaningfully buffer serum cortisol short-term; cycle ~30 days on, then 2–4 weeks off. **[medium]** for cortisol effect; **[low]** for long-term use
- NMN/NR/resveratrol longevity claims: mechanistically interesting (NAD+ biology) but human longevity/healthspan evidence is weak; Huberman self-experiments (NMN 1–2 g sublingual), Attia is skeptical of the class. **[low]**
- Grape-seed extract 400–800 mg/day with a meal — creator's personal stack; evidence "reasonably strong" per Huberman but not consensus. **[low/medium]**

### Vision, learning, focus & motivation
- Children/people getting ≥2 h/day outdoor daylight have lower myopia incidence (large clinical trials); does not require avoiding near-work the whole time. **[high]**
- Balance near-work with distance viewing; periodic focus relaxation/exercises a few days/week may help. **[medium]**
- Ultradian focus blocks (~90 min) with ~10–15 min ramp-in; you can't "force" deep focus on a clock. **[medium]**
- Deliberate task-switching transitions (60–90 s up to 5–10 min) reduce the cost of shifting attention. **[medium]**
- NSDR/yoga-nidra 10–30 min raises striatal dopamine and can partially offset lost sleep. **[medium]** (one small dopamine study often cited; effect likely real but evidence thin)
- ~10–13 min/day focused-breathing meditation improves attention (Wendy Suzuki / NYU data). **[medium/high]**

## Actionable protocols

**Morning circadian anchor**
- Get outside for direct sunlight to the eyes within 30–60 min of waking: ~5 min clear, ~10 min light cloud, ~20–30 min heavy overcast. No sunglasses (corrective lenses fine); never stare at painfully bright light. Don't rely on light through glass.
- Optionally raise core temperature: cold shower 1–3 min and/or light exercise/walk (which also delivers the sunlight).
- Caveat: winter/dark climates — supplement with a ~900-lux LED panel 90 min after waking; it's an aid, not a full replacement.

**Caffeine timing**
- Delay first caffeine ~90–120 min after waking. Keep typical intake 100–300 mg. Avoid caffeine within ~8–12 h of bed; if any, <100 mg after 4 p.m.
- For a tolerance reset, abstain ~4 days to 1 week.

**Evening wind-down**
- After sunset: dim/avoid bright (especially overhead) artificial light 10 p.m.–4 a.m.; dim screens; red light if awake mid-cycle.
- Sauna/hot bath ≤20–30 min earlier in the evening to trigger compensatory cooling; keep the bedroom cool and layer blankets.
- No large meals within ~2 h of bed; avoid alcohol/THC within ~8 h.
- Optional sleep stack 30–60 min pre-bed: magnesium L-threonate ~145 mg, apigenin ~50 mg, theanine 100–400 mg. Caveat: drop Mg if it causes GI upset; drop theanine if dreams are disruptive; talk to a physician before changing supplements.

**Jet lag / shift adjustment (temperature minimum)**
- Estimate temp-min ≈ wake time minus 2 h. To advance (sleep/wake earlier): light + exercise + caffeine *after* temp-min. To delay: do those *before* temp-min. Pre-adapt 1–2 days before travel.

**Exercise**
- ~150–200 min/week zone-2 cardio; add HIIT (e.g., 20 s hard / 10 s easy × ~8). Cap sessions ~60–75 min. Full rest day after 2 consecutive resistance-training days.

**NSDR / focus**
- NSDR/yoga-nidra 10–30 min once daily (especially after poor sleep) for dopamine restoration and recovery.
- Work in ~90-min ultradian blocks; allow ~10–15 min to drop into focus; insert 60 s–10 min transition periods between tasks.

**Cold exposure**
- Cold immersion/shower aiming for a few minutes total (1–3 min typical; build gradually; generally ≤5–6 min). "Uncomfortable but safe." Don't start at 10 min; don't stay chilled afterward beyond ~10–30 min.

**Supplements (use judgment)**
- Collagen 15–30 g/day ≥2 weeks for skin. Protein ~1 g/lb/day. Ashwagandha 2×250–300 mg/day, cycle ~30 days on / 2–4 weeks off. Treat NMN/NR/resveratrol and grape-seed extract as experimental, not evidence-backed for longevity.

## Contested / where creator framing outruns evidence

- **NMN / NR / resveratrol for longevity** — presented by Huberman as a worthwhile personal stack (e.g., NMN 1–2 g sublingual); human healthspan/longevity evidence is weak-to-absent. Attia is openly skeptical of the same class. **Treat as low confidence.**
- **Precise supplement dosages** (Mg threonate 145 mg, apigenin 50 mg, theanine 100–400 mg, grape-seed 400–800 mg) — stated with precision that exceeds the underlying (often small or personal) evidence. The numbers are starting points, not validated optima.
- **90–120 min caffeine delay to "avoid the afternoon crash"** — mechanistically plausible (adenosine/cortisol interaction) but not strongly demonstrated in controlled trials; popularized largely through these podcasts.
- **Exact light-duration thresholds** (5/10/20–30 min by cloud cover; ≥2 h outdoors for myopia is better supported than the morning durations) — practical heuristics, not trial-derived cutoffs. Huberman himself repeatedly notes he "can't give an absolute prescriptive."
- **Temperature-minimum = wake − 2 h** — a useful simplification of a real circadian marker, but the exact offset varies between individuals.
- **NSDR raising dopamine** — rests heavily on a single small study; the effect is plausible but the evidence base is thin relative to how often it's cited.
- **COVID-19 episodes (2020)** — hydroxychloroquine discussion and some vitamin-D framing are time-stamped to early-pandemic knowledge and do not reflect later consensus; read as historical. **Do not treat as current.**
- **Zone-2-centric cardio prescription** — the ~150 min/week activity floor is well established; the specific zone-2 emphasis and weekly architecture are reasonable but reflect Attia's framing more than settled consensus.
