# DR-011: Predictive processing & free-energy: empirical support vs overreach

## Executive summary

Predictive processing (PP) spans a layered set of claims that must be evaluated separately rather than as a single package. From strongest to weakest empirical footing:

1. **The brain is predictive / anticipatory** (high confidence). Prediction-related signals — mismatch responses, expectation effects, surprise responses — are robust and replicated across species, modalities, and methods. That the cortex anticipates input is essentially uncontested.

2. **The brain implements *predictive coding* specifically** — i.e., dedicated prediction-error units, hierarchical generative models, feedback carrying predictions and feedforward carrying errors (medium confidence). The architectural prediction (errors in superficial layers, predictions in deep layers) has recently received its first direct human laminar support (7T fMRI, V1) and converging mouse cellular evidence (L2/3 mismatch neurons). But the canonical microcircuit mapping (Bastos 2012) is still largely a theoretical correspondence, and many "prediction-error" signals are confounded by adaptation, novelty, surprise, and attention.

3. **Precision-weighting is implemented by cholinergic/noradrenergic gain control** (medium-low confidence). The conceptual mapping (ACh = sensory/expected precision, NE/LC = environmental volatility/unexpected uncertainty, both via synaptic gain on error units) is coherent and supported by suggestive pharmacology and pupillometry, but causal, receptor-specific, layer-specific human evidence is thin and the pupil↔LC link is noisier than often assumed.

4. **The Free Energy Principle (FEP) as a grand unifying theory of all self-organizing/biological systems** (low confidence / not an empirical claim). Friston himself frames FEP as a near-tautological mathematical principle (like least action), not a falsifiable hypothesis. Critics across philosophy of science argue its generality comes from tautology, not unification, and that it is silent on biophysics. This is where creator/popular framing most outruns the evidence.

The defensible line: **predictive coding is a productive, partially-confirmed process theory of cortical computation; the FEP is a unifying mathematical framework whose specific neuroscientific content is exactly the (falsifiable) predictive-coding/active-inference process theories — not the principle itself.** Treat "the brain minimizes free energy, therefore X" arguments as heuristic, not evidence.

## Key claims & evidence (with confidence + URLs)

### Claim 1 — Prediction/expectation signals exist and are robust (mismatch, expectation, surprise). HIGH
Mismatch negativity (MMN), repetition suppression, and oddball/surprise responses are reliably observed in EEG/MEG and single-unit work; unexpected stimuli evoke larger responses than expected ones. These are the empirical backbone PP must explain.
- Walsh et al. 2020, *Ann NY Acad Sci* (review of neurophysiological evidence): https://nyaspubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/nyas.14321
- Millidge, Seth, Buckley 2021, theoretical/experimental review: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2107.12979

### Claim 2 — Direct cellular evidence for prediction-error neurons (mouse V1). MEDIUM
A subset of L2/3 neurons in mouse V1 respond selectively to the *absence* of expected visual flow/stimulus (mismatch / negative prediction error), consistent with dedicated error units in superficial layers.
- Keller & Mrsic-Flogel 2018, *Neuron*, "Predictive Processing: A Canonical Cortical Computation": https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6400266/ ; PDF: https://discovery.ucl.ac.uk/id/eprint/10064516/3/Keller_Mrsic-Flogel.pdf
Caveat: mismatch responses can also reflect motor/locomotion-visual coupling and adaptation; whether these are true hierarchical Bayesian error units vs. context-dependent signals is debated.

### Claim 3 — Human laminar dissociation: errors in superficial layers, predictions in deep layers. MEDIUM (newly emerging, replicates a core architectural prediction)
7T layer-fMRI in human V1: unexpected (prediction-error) signals are decodable in *superficial* laminae, while expected/prediction-related stimulus-specific activity appears in *deep* layers — matching the Bastos/Rao-Ballard architecture.
- Aitken et al. 2024, *Current Biology*, "Predictions and errors are distinctly represented across V1 layers": https://www.cell.com/current-biology/fulltext/S0960-9822(24)00471-8 ; PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38697110/
This is among the strongest *direct* human tests of the architecture, but it is a small, recent literature with known superficial-bias confounds in gradient-echo 7T fMRI; independent replication still limited.

### Claim 4 — Canonical microcircuit mapping (laminar + spectral) is a theoretical correspondence, not proven. MEDIUM-LOW
Bastos et al. 2012 map predictive coding onto cortical layers: superficial pyramidal cells = prediction errors (more gamma), deep pyramidal cells = predictions (more beta/alpha). Feedforward (gamma) carries errors up; feedback (beta) carries predictions down. Gamma/beta directionality has correlational support; the computational role assignment is inferred, not directly demonstrated.
- Bastos et al. 2012, *Neuron*, "Canonical Microcircuits for Predictive Coding": https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3777738/

### Claim 5 — Precision-weighting maps onto acetylcholine (sensory/expected precision via gain). MEDIUM-LOW
PP theory: precision (inverse variance, confidence in a signal) is implemented as synaptic gain on error units, with ACh boosting the gain of (expected/sensory) prediction errors. Direct microiontophoretic ACh application in rat auditory cortex increases the *variance/precision* of mismatch and prediction-error responses (especially infragranular), affecting error responses but not repetition suppression.
- Pérez-González et al. 2024, *eLife*, "Acetylcholine modulates the precision of prediction error in the auditory cortex": https://elifesciences.org/articles/91475
Caveats (authors' own): exogenous ACh may exceed physiological levels; anesthetized prep; no receptor-specific or genetic manipulation — so suggestive, not causal-in-vivo-behaving.

### Claim 6 — Precision/uncertainty maps onto noradrenaline (LC-NE) and pupil-linked arousal. MEDIUM
Pupil-linked arousal (a proxy for LC-NE) tracks environmental volatility, uncertainty-driven learning-rate adjustments, and (precision-weighted) prediction errors — consistent with NE signaling "unexpected uncertainty"/volatility and gating belief updating.
- Nassar et al. 2012, *Nat Neurosci*, "Rational regulation of learning dynamics by pupil-linked arousal systems": https://www.nature.com/articles/nn.3130
- Task-evoked pupil tracks precision-weighted PE and learning rate: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-022-26544-w
- Pupil + fMRI BOLD in human LC: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/hbm.22466
Critical caveat: pupil diameter is **not** an accurate moment-to-moment readout of LC spiking — the pupil↔LC mapping is loose, weakening strong "NE = precision" claims built on pupillometry alone.
- Megemont, McBurney-Lin, Yang 2022, *eLife*, "Pupil diameter is not an accurate real-time readout of locus coeruleus activity": https://elifesciences.org/articles/70510

### Claim 7 — Attention as precision optimization. MEDIUM
Selective attention is modeled as increasing the expected precision (synaptic gain) of behaviorally relevant prediction errors; attention promotes the neural encoding of prediction errors, and DCM studies of attention are consistent with gain modulation of error units.
- "Attention Promotes the Neural Encoding of Prediction Errors" (preprint): https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/522185.full.pdf
- Friston DCM attention study (functional anatomy of attention): https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3845206/
This reframing is elegant and data-consistent, but "attention = precision" is partly a redescription; it does not yet uniquely outcompete classical attentional-gain accounts.

### Claim 8 — The FEP as a universal/grand-unifying principle is (by its own framing) not falsifiable. LOW (as an empirical claim)
Friston frames FEP as a near-tautological mathematical truth analogous to Hamilton's principle of least action — a *framework*, not a testable hypothesis. Philosophers argue its apparent generality is a product of tautology rather than genuine unification, that it is silent on the nervous system's biophysics, and that explanatory pluralism (many models) is preferable to one grand theory. The good science (predictive coding / active inference *process theories*) is separable from the questionable philosophy (FEP-as-metaphysics, "Markovian monism").
- Colombo & Wright 2021, *Synthese*, "First principles in the life sciences: the free-energy principle, organicism, and mechanism": https://philpapers.org/rec/COLFPI
- Colombo & Wright, "Explanatory Pluralism: An Unrewarding Prediction Error for Free Energy Theorists": https://philpapers.org/rec/COLEPA-6
- Sánchez-Cañizares 2021, *Entropy*, "The FEP: Good Science and Questionable Philosophy in a Grand Unifying Theory": https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7922226/

## Conflicts & uncertainty

- **Prediction error vs. adaptation/novelty/surprise confounds.** Many "expectation suppression" / "prediction error" effects are confounded with low-level neuronal adaptation, stimulus novelty, surprise, and attention. Feuerriegel et al. and others show cortical adaptation can occur without expectation suppression, and some EEG studies find stimulus expectations do *not* modulate visual ERPs in well-controlled probabilistic-cueing designs. This is the central live empirical dispute: how much of the PP evidence base survives proper controls.
  - https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2023.04.05.535778v2.full
  - https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0010945220302847
- **Architecture: real error units vs. emergent signals.** Even sympathetic reviewers (Walsh 2020; the *empirical status* review) conclude existing evidence offers only *modest* support for the strong claim of *separate, dedicated* prediction and prediction-error populations; much is consistent with PP but not uniquely diagnostic of it.
  - https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0149763423004426 (Bogacz/Spratling-adjacent "empirical status" review; abstract accessible)
- **Neuromodulator mapping is cartoon-level.** The clean ACh=expected-precision / NE=unexpected-uncertainty / DA=confidence scheme is heuristic. Pharmacology is mostly preclinical/anesthetized; the pupil↔LC proxy is unreliable; receptor-specific causal human evidence is largely absent.
- **FEP falsifiability.** Proponents say FEP is "almost tautological" by design and should be judged as a framework; critics say that makes the *principle* (as opposed to its process theories) scientifically empty. Both can be right: the principle is not falsifiable, but the predictive-coding/active-inference models it motivates are.
- **Laminar fMRI confounds.** Human deep/superficial dissociations (Aitken 2024) are exciting but rest on draining-vein/superficial-bias-prone gradient-echo BOLD and small samples; treat as promising, not settled.

## What not to absorb yet

- "The brain *is* a Bayesian/free-energy machine" stated as established fact. Bayesian-*consistent* behavior and neural signals ≠ demonstrated Bayesian *implementation*; many results are also explained by adaptation or by non-Bayesian heuristics.
- "Acetylcholine encodes precision / norepinephrine encodes volatility" as a settled mechanistic fact. It is a plausible, partially-supported mapping built largely on preclinical pharmacology and an unreliable pupil-LC proxy — not causal human evidence.
- "The FEP explains life, mind, agency, and consciousness." This is creator/popular-framing overreach; the FEP is (by Friston's own account) a non-falsifiable mathematical principle. Do not record it as an empirical neuroscience finding.
- Strong claims that the canonical microcircuit (gamma=feedforward error, beta=feedback prediction) is *proven*. It is a well-motivated, correlationally-supported theoretical mapping.
- Treating expectation-suppression / repetition-suppression results as clean prediction-error evidence without checking for adaptation, novelty, surprise, and attention controls.
