# DR-002 Reconciliation: Psychedelic & Rapid-Acting Antidepressants

Cross-check of two independent deep-research reports answering DR-002.

- **CDR** = ChatGPT report (`raw_reports/chatgpt/DR-002-CDR.md`)
- **Agent** = Autonomous agent report (`raw_reports/agent/DR-002.md`)

---

## Agreements (both reports make the claim — high confidence)

1. **Esketamine (Spravato) is FDA-approved for TRD; one acute Phase 3 (TRANSFORM-2) was positive, two (TRANSFORM-1 fixed-dose, TRANSFORM-3 elderly) missed their primary endpoint.** Both frame the acute efficacy signal as "real but modest/fragile."
2. **SUSTAIN-1 randomized-withdrawal supports esketamine maintenance:** continuing esketamine delayed relapse vs placebo, HR ≈ 0.49 (remitters) and ≈ 0.30 (responders). Both note this is an enrichment/withdrawal design.
3. **Long-term esketamine durability data are open-label** (SUSTAIN-2 and/or SUSTAIN-3), i.e., safety/maintenance context rather than controlled efficacy.
4. **Racemic IV ketamine produces rapid but short-lived antidepressant effects** (single dose lasting roughly days to ~1 week); durability requires repeated dosing.
5. **Ketamine effect estimates are threatened by functional unblinding / comparator choice;** both treat the headline single-saline-controlled numbers as likely inflated.
6. **COMPASS COMP360 psilocybin Phase 2b (233 pts, TRD, NEJM 2022): 25 mg beat 1 mg by −6.6 MADRS points at week 3** (p<0.001). Both cite the same trial and effect size.
7. **Psilocybin remission ~29% in the 25 mg arm; durability limited — only ~20% sustained response at week 12.** Acute signal attenuates.
8. **Carhart-Harris psilocybin-vs-escitalopram (NEJM 2021) FAILED its primary endpoint:** QIDS-SR-16 between-group difference 2.0 points, p=0.17; some secondaries numerically favored psilocybin but cannot establish superiority. Both warn against "psilocybin beats SSRIs" framing.
9. **Psilocybin trials carry a serious adverse-event signal including suicidal ideation/behavior/self-injury across all arms, most concerning in the 25 mg arm;** causality vs underlying TRD unresolved.
10. **MDMA-assisted therapy showed large Phase 3 PTSD effect sizes (MAPP1 2021, MAPP2 2023).**
11. **FDA declined MDMA-assisted therapy (CRL, Aug 2024); advisory committee voted 9–2 that efficacy was not shown and 10–1 that benefits did not outweigh risks; FDA requested another randomized Phase 3 trial.**
12. **The MDMA rejection was multifactorial:** severe functional unblinding, expectancy/selection bias (~40% of participants had prior MDMA use), missing/unreliable safety data (abuse-potential, cardiovascular), inadequate durability evidence, inconsistent therapy delivery, and trial-conduct/misconduct concerns. Both explicitly reject the "FDA rejected it because the drug doesn't work" simplification.
13. **Functional unblinding / expectancy is the dominant cross-cutting methodological threat across psychedelic RCTs** (both cite Muthukumaraswamy 2021); both warn against absorbing raw effect sizes as clean placebo-corrected estimates.
14. **Mechanistic "neuroplasticity/rewiring heals depression" claims are not established as the clinical mechanism in humans** and should not be absorbed as settled.

## Conflicts

No direct factual contradictions were found — the reports are highly convergent. The items below are differences in emphasis, evidentiary basis, or numeric specificity rather than outright disagreement; listed for transparency.

| Point | CDR says | Agent says |
|---|---|---|
| AdComm vote ordering/notation | "9–2 that efficacy had not been shown" and "10–1 that benefits did not outweigh risks" | "voted 2–9 (effectiveness) and 1–10 (benefit/risk) against" — same votes, opposite numeral order (no factual conflict, but a reader could misread direction) |
| Primary lever undercutting ketamine's apparent effect | Comparator/meta-analytic: midazolam vs saline (d≈0.7 vs d≈1.8, Wilkinson) and a 2024 meta-analysis attributing ~72% (sensitivity ~62%) of response to placebo | A single surgical-anesthesia masking trial (Nature Mental Health 2023) showing no significant ketamine advantage when dissociation was concealed; flags it needs replication |
| Esketamine monotherapy efficacy magnitude | Detailed: 2025 Janik monotherapy study, day-28 LS mean diffs −5.1 (56 mg) / −6.8 (84 mg), effect sizes 0.48 / 0.63, but only a 4-week study | Mentions only that esketamine "later won approval as monotherapy" — no numbers |
| Carhart-Harris secondary outcomes | Reports QIDS response 70% vs 48%, remission 57% vs 28% (uncorrected for multiplicity) | States secondaries "numerically favored psilocybin" without the figures |

## Unique to CDR (ChatGPT)

- **Zarate 2006 crossover and Murrough 2013 active-placebo specifics:** ketamine separated from placebo at ~110 min; Murrough −7.95 MADRS at 24h, response 64% vs 28% (midazolam).
- **2025 esketamine monotherapy (Janik) numeric detail** (−5.1 / −6.8 at day 28; effect sizes 0.48 / 0.63; separation by 24h).
- **Quantitative placebo-response meta-analysis:** ~72% of ketamine/esketamine response attributable to placebo (sensitivity ~62%; 28–40% drug-attributable).
- **2026 human AMPAR PET evidence (Nakajima, Molecular Psychiatry)** linking ketamine-induced cell-surface AMPA receptor changes to symptom improvement — direct human mechanistic support.
- **Detailed mechanism discussion:** NMDA→AMPA→BDNF/mTOR cascade as plausible-but-incompletely-resolved in humans.
- **(2R,6R)-hydroxynorketamine (HNK) status:** provisional; safe in phase 1 but no clinical antidepressant efficacy demonstrated.
- **Opioid-system controversy (Williams 2018 naltrexone; non-replication):** opioid signaling possibly modulatory/permissive, not opioid-primary.
- **"Dissociation not required for efficacy"** argument (naltrexone blocked response while sparing dissociation; 2025 monotherapy similar response with/without dissociative AEs).
- **2026 JAMA Psychiatry blinding-integrity systematic review (Orsini):** only 29.5% of psychedelic RCTs assessed blinding; >90% blinding failure (psilocybin/LSD/ayahuasca), >85% (inert-placebo MDMA), ketamine assessed in only 17.9%.
- **COMPASS Phase 3 status (COMP005 met primary endpoint 2025; COMP006 26-week data expected 2H 2026)** flagged as sponsor top-line, not regulator-grade — explicitly "do not absorb as settled."
- **MDMA CRL granular detail:** MAPP1/MAPP2 CAPS-5 effect sizes (−11.86, −8.91), ~90% drug / ~75% placebo guess accuracy, MPLONG durability inadequacy, "do not record favourable experiences as AEs" instruction, recommended low-dose MDMA control arm and third-party audit.
- **Carhart-Harris open-label feasibility (n=20) study** noted as hypothesis-generating only.

## Unique to Agent

- **ESCAPE-TRD (NEJM 2023):** esketamine superior to quetiapine XR head-to-head for TRD remission/maintenance — a positive active-comparator trial not mentioned by CDR.
- **SUSTAIN-3 specifics:** mean exposure ~42.9 months, persistent MADRS improvement, no new safety signals.
- **SUSTAIN-1 relapse rates:** ~26.7% on esketamine vs ~45.3% on placebo.
- **Surgical-anesthesia masking trial (Nature Mental Health 2023)** as the central "expectancy implicated" ketamine evidence.
- **MAPP1 headline response rates:** ~67% no longer met PTSD criteria, ~88% clinically meaningful reduction; flagged as confounded/uncorrected.
- **Explicit "expectancy: bias or feature?" paradigmatic dispute** (MAPS/Lykos view vs FDA/methodologist view), with a counter-view citation arguing expectation is intrinsic to the therapy.
- **In-progress independent IPD meta-analysis (medRxiv 2025 preprint)** on esketamine, plus critique that withdrawal design may exaggerate maintenance via discontinuation effects.
- **Aday et al. 2022** as an additional expectancy-confound methods citation.

## Verdict

The two reports are **strongly convergent** — there are no genuine factual contradictions, only differences in depth and sourcing. Every load-bearing claim is independently corroborated: esketamine's "1-of-3 positive acute pivotals + withdrawal-design maintenance + open-label long-term" profile; ketamine's rapid-but-short-lived effect; the COMPASS −6.6-point week-3 result with ~20% week-12 durability; the null Carhart-Harris primary endpoint (p=0.17); psilocybin's all-arm suicidality signal; and the multifactorial MDMA rejection (9–2 / 10–1 votes, unblinding, ~40% prior use, safety gaps, conduct concerns). These claims now warrant **higher confidence** given two-source agreement. The pervasive functional-unblinding/expectancy confound — and the explicit caution against treating raw psychedelic effect sizes as clean — is the shared spine of both reports and should be treated as the highest-confidence meta-finding. Claims warranting **lower or held confidence** because only one report carries them (and they are sponsor-grade, single-study, or mechanistic): COMPASS Phase 3 COMP005/COMP006 readouts (CDR-only, sponsor top-line); the surgical-anesthesia ketamine masking trial as evidence the effect is "mostly placebo" (agent-only, single unreplicated study); the AMPAR PET mechanism and HNK/opioid mechanistic claims (CDR-only, evolving); and ESCAPE-TRD superiority over quetiapine (agent-only — credible but uncorroborated here). The one numeric detail a knowledge base should standardize carefully is the MDMA AdComm vote, which both report identically (9 against efficacy, 10 against benefit/risk) but with inverted numeral ordering. Net: a high-convergence pair where the agent adds an active-comparator and a masking-trial angle, while CDR adds mechanism, blinding-meta, and regulatory granularity; merging both yields a more complete, well-hedged record than either alone.
