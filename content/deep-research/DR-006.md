# DR-006: Testosterone boosters & TRT cardiovascular/fertility safety

## Executive summary

The strongest RCT-grade lifestyle lever on endogenous testosterone is **weight loss in overweight/obese men**: diet-induced loss of ~10% body weight raises total testosterone by roughly ~2-3 nmol/L (~85 ng/dL), and bariatric surgery (~30% loss) by up to ~8.7 nmol/L (~250 ng/dL), via reversal of obesity-associated hypogonadotropic hypogonadism. **Sleep** matters but the data are mixed: one widely cited crossover study found ~10-15% lower daytime testosterone after one week of 5-hour sleep, while a later RCT found no significant change — so "sleep restriction tanks testosterone" is directionally plausible but not robustly replicated. **Exercise** has a negligible effect on *resting* testosterone in meta-analysis (transient post-exercise spikes do not translate into durable baseline gains).

For supplements, the marketing badly outruns the evidence. **Ashwagandha** has the most RCT support (small trials show ~+90-120 ng/dL over placebo in stressed/overweight men), but trials are small, industry-funded, heterogeneous, and increasingly shadowed by a real **hepatotoxicity signal** (multiple RUCAM-confirmed "probable" herb-induced liver injury cases, one transplant, and a 2024 Australian TGA safety alert). **Tongkat Ali** has modest RCT signal in older/low-T men (Physta multicentre and training studies). **Zinc** raises testosterone mainly when correcting a deficiency, not in replete men. **Boron** evidence is thin and inconsistent in men. **Fadogia agrestis** has essentially **zero human trials** — popular framing (largely from podcast/creator culture) rests on rodent studies that also flag dose-dependent testicular toxicity.

On TRT safety, **TRAVERSE** (NEJM 2023; n=5,246 hypogonadal men with high CV risk) found transdermal testosterone gel **noninferior to placebo for MACE** (HR 0.96, 95% CI 0.78-1.17), but with **higher rates of atrial fibrillation, pulmonary embolism, and acute kidney injury**. TRT reliably suppresses spermatogenesis (functions as a male contraceptive; azoospermia common, mostly reversible over 6-12 months) and raises hematocrit (monitor, keep <54%).

## Key claims & evidence (with confidence + URLs)

### Lifestyle levers

1. **Weight loss reverses obesity-related low testosterone, dose-dependently — HIGH.**
Meta-analysis: diet-associated weight loss (mean ~9.8%) increased total T by ~2.9 nmol/L (~84 ng/dL); surgical weight loss (~32%) by ~8.7 nmol/L (~251 ng/dL). Roughly ~0.6% T gain per 1 kg lost. This is the best-evidenced lifestyle lever.
- https://academic.oup.com/ejendo/article-abstract/168/6/829/6659681
- https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/andr.13484
- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39840189/

2. **Short sleep lowers testosterone — MEDIUM (mixed/contested magnitude).**
The classic crossover (Leproult & Van Cauter, UChicago) reported ~10-15% lower daytime T after 1 week of 5 h sleep in young men. A later randomized study (Sleep Health 2019) found sleep restriction did **not** significantly change plasma testosterone. Direction plausible; magnitude not robustly replicated.
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4445839/
- https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S2352721819301391

3. **Exercise has negligible effect on resting/basal testosterone — HIGH.**
Meta-analysis in sedentary men: effect of training on resting total T was negligible and did not differ by mode, age, or BMI; no significant effect on free T. In older men, resistance training does not significantly raise basal T. Acute post-workout spikes are transient and do not durably raise baseline. (Resistance training may modestly raise T in younger subgroups.)
- https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6339914/
- https://commons.und.edu/theses/3117/

### Supplements (trial evidence vs marketing)

4. **Ashwagandha modestly raises testosterone in small RCTs, but evidence is low-quality and heterogeneous — MEDIUM.**
RCTs report ~+90-120 ng/dL vs placebo (e.g., +96 ng/dL vs +18 with 600 mg/day root extract for 8 weeks in overweight/stressed men). Doses and standardization vary widely (240-675 mg/day); many trials are industry-funded and small. A systematic review concludes high-quality data are too sparse for definitive conclusions.
- https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/hsr2.741
- https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2161831322001028
- https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/ptr.70128

5. **Ashwagandha carries a real hepatotoxicity signal — MEDIUM (emerging, replicated case-level).**
Multiple case reports/series of herb-induced liver injury; RUCAM scoring rated cases "probable" (e.g., scores of 7). Pattern mixed/cholestatic/hepatocellular; latency days-to-months; most recover on discontinuation but at least one liver transplant reported. Iceland + US DILI Network case series and a Feb 2024 Australian TGA safety alert (12 reports, 4 hospitalizations) reinforce the signal. Proposed mechanism: withanone-induced DNA damage.
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10459262/
- https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/ccr3.7078
- https://scholarworks.indianapolis.iu.edu/items/ce46952a-4482-4275-ac7e-725e5adab9a8
- https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2666027X21000074

6. **Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia) shows modest testosterone benefit in older/low-T men — MEDIUM.**
RCTs: 12 weeks of 100-200 mg increased total T in older men with low baseline T, with 200 mg improving strength; a 6-month RCT showed erectile-function benefit (largest with concurrent training). Physta standardized aqueous extract multicentre RCT reported T and quality-of-life improvement in ageing men. Effects modest; some trials industry-linked.
- https://www.maturitas.org/article/S0378-5122(20)30449-7/abstract
- https://foodandnutritionresearch.net/index.php/fnr/article/view/5647
- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33541567/

7. **Zinc raises testosterone mainly when correcting deficiency, not in replete men — MEDIUM.**
Systematic review links serum zinc to testosterone; supplementation helps in zinc-deficient/hypogonadal men. But a trial in non-deficient cardiac-surgery patients found no significant T difference. Net: correct a deficiency, don't expect supraphysiologic boosting in replete men.
- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36577241/
- https://journals.lww.com/ajandrology/fulltext/2017/19030/the_effectiveness_of_zinc_supplementation_in_men.3.aspx
- https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC11892516/

8. **Boron evidence in men is thin and inconsistent — LOW.**
Some signals (e.g., T rise in postmenopausal women at 3 mg/day), but a 7-week trial of 2.5 mg boron in 19 male bodybuilders found no significant testosterone effect. Insufficient high-quality human data in men.
- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8508192/
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4712861/

9. **Fadogia agrestis has no human trials; rodent data flag testicular toxicity — LOW (popular framing far outruns evidence).**
No human RCTs exist. Claims trace to a small number of rodent studies and creator/podcast popularity (notably Huberman-adjacent discussion), not clinical evidence. Animal data show dose-dependent toxicity to testicular structure/enzymes; safety in humans is unestablished.
- https://www.droracle.ai/articles/164019/is-there-evidence-linking-fadogia-agrestis-to-increased-testosterone
- https://biologyinsights.com/does-fadogia-agrestis-work-a-look-at-the-science/

### TRT cardiovascular / hematocrit / fertility

10. **TRAVERSE: TRT noninferior to placebo for MACE in high-CV-risk hypogonadal men — HIGH.**
n=5,246 men (mean age ~63, ~70% type 2 diabetes), transdermal 1.62% testosterone gel titrated to 350-750 ng/dL. Primary MACE HR 0.96 (95% CI 0.78-1.17), P<0.001 for noninferiority over ~22 months.
- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37326322/
- https://www.nejm.org/doi/pdf/10.1056/NEJMoa2215025

11. **TRAVERSE flagged higher atrial fibrillation, pulmonary embolism, and acute kidney injury with TRT — HIGH (signal), MEDIUM (mechanism/causality).**
The testosterone group had higher incidence of AF, PE, and AKI. A European Expert Panel notes prior meta-analysis of 106 RCTs found AF risk not statistically significant (MH-OR 1.44 [0.46-4.46]) and that arrhythmia endpoints in TRAVERSE were underpowered; generalizability limited (old, obese, diabetic cohort; ~22-month follow-up; COVID-era confounding).
- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37326322/
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12670475/

12. **TRT raises hematocrit/erythrocytosis; guideline target is to keep Hct <54% — HIGH.**
European panel recommends monitoring Hct every 3-6 months in year one then annually, keeping <54%, with dose reduction/temporary discontinuation if exceeded. Registry data (HEAT) suggest modest Hct elevations did not clearly correlate with thromboembolic events, though this remains debated.
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12670475/

13. **Exogenous testosterone suppresses spermatogenesis and acts as a contraceptive; mostly reversible — HIGH.**
TRT raises serum T, suppressing LH/FSH and intratesticular T, causing oligo/azoospermia (azoospermia in a majority within months in contraceptive studies). Recovery occurs in ~64-84% (some series ~100%) after cessation, median ~110 days, most by 6-12 months but occasionally up to ~2 years. hCG (and/or FSH) co-administration can preserve intratesticular T and fertility for men wanting children.
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6305868/
- https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/17562872221105017
- https://www.fertstert.org/article/S0015-0282(10)01624-9/fulltext

## Conflicts & uncertainty

- **Sleep & testosterone:** The famous ~10-15% drop (UChicago crossover) is contradicted by a later randomized study showing no significant change. Magnitude and reproducibility are genuinely uncertain.
- **TRAVERSE AF/PE/AKI signal:** Statistically present in TRAVERSE but endpoints were secondary/underpowered, and a large prior meta-analysis did not find significant AF risk. Whether these are causal or chance/cohort artifacts (old, diabetic, COVID-era population) is unresolved.
- **Hematocrit and clot risk:** Guidelines treat Hct >54% as actionable, yet registry data suggest modest elevations may not translate into more thromboembolic events. The clinical threshold is precautionary rather than firmly outcome-validated.
- **Ashwagandha benefit vs harm:** Modest T/sexual-function benefits come from small, often industry-funded RCTs; the hepatotoxicity signal comes from uncontrolled case reports (confounded by supplement quality/adulteration). Both the benefit magnitude and the true hepatotoxicity incidence are uncertain.
- **Supplement quality/standardization:** Eurycoma and Withania extracts vary enormously in standardization and contamination, making cross-study comparison and real-world extrapolation hazardous.

## What not to absorb yet

- **"Fadogia agrestis is a proven natural T-booster."** No human trials; rodent data show testicular toxicity. This is creator/podcast-driven hype, not evidence — do not absorb as established.
- **"Tongkat Ali + Fadogia stacks reliably raise testosterone in healthy young men."** Tongkat Ali RCTs are mostly in older/low-T men with modest effects; no quality data support stacks or healthy-young-male efficacy.
- **"Boron meaningfully raises testosterone in men."** Inconsistent; a male RCT found no effect. Treat as low-confidence.
- **"Zinc boosts testosterone in everyone."** Benefit appears confined to deficiency correction; no clear supraphysiologic boost in replete men.
- **"TRT is fully cardiovascular-safe."** TRAVERSE supports MACE noninferiority but flagged AF/PE/AKI; do not over-generalize to younger/healthier or long-term (>2 yr) use, and do not ignore the arrhythmia/thrombosis signals.
- **"TRT doesn't affect fertility."** False — it predictably suppresses spermatogenesis; men desiring fertility need hCG/FSH-sparing strategies or alternatives (clomiphene, etc.).
- **Specific TRAVERSE per-arm event counts** for AF/PE/AKI were not captured from the abstract here — cite the full NEJM text before quoting exact numbers.
