# DR-002: Psychedelic & rapid-acting antidepressants: ketamine, psilocybin, MDMA

## Executive summary

Rapid-acting and psychedelic compounds occupy very different rungs of the evidence ladder despite often being lumped together in popular framing.

- **Esketamine (Spravato)** has the strongest regulatory footing: it is FDA-approved (2019) for treatment-resistant depression (TRD) on the basis of one positive short-term flexible-dose Phase 3 trial (TRANSFORM-2), a positive randomized-withdrawal relapse-prevention trial (SUSTAIN-1), and long-term open-label data (SUSTAIN-2/3). It later won approval as monotherapy and showed superiority over quetiapine XR (ESCAPE-TRD, NEJM 2023). Two of three acute pivotal trials (TRANSFORM-1 and TRANSFORM-3 in the elderly) did **not** hit their primary endpoint, so the efficacy signal is real but modest and somewhat fragile.
- **Racemic IV/SC ketamine** produces rapid but **short-lived** antidepressant effects (single-dose benefit averaging ~1 week); durability requires repeated dosing, and effect estimates are threatened by near-total functional unblinding.
- **Psilocybin** has one large positive trial (COMPASS COMP360 Phase 2b, NEJM 2022: 25 mg beat 1 mg by −6.6 MADRS points at week 3, p<0.001) but a head-to-head comparison against escitalopram (Carhart-Harris, NEJM 2021) **failed its primary endpoint** (QIDS-SR-16 between-group difference 2.0 points, p=0.17). Durability is limited: only ~20% sustained response at week 12 in COMPASS.
- **MDMA-assisted therapy** showed large PTSD effect sizes in two Phase 3 trials (MAPP1 2021, MAPP2 2023), but the **FDA declined approval in August 2024** (Complete Response Letter to Lykos). The advisory committee voted 2–9 (effectiveness) and 1–10 (benefit/risk) against, citing severe functional unblinding, expectancy bias, inconsistent/unverifiable therapy delivery, missing abuse-potential and cardiovascular safety data, allegations of trial misconduct, and unclear durability. FDA requested an additional Phase 3 trial.

The single most important cross-cutting caveat: **the expectancy / functional-unblinding confound is severe across all psychedelics** (psilocybin and MDMA especially, ketamine substantially). The unmistakable subjective drug effect means patients and raters can nearly always guess assignment, which likely inflates reported effect sizes. This is contested in interpretation (Lykos/MAPS argue patient expectation is intrinsic to the therapy, not removable bias), but it is the central reason regulators and methodologists discount headline numbers.

## Key claims & evidence (with confidence + URLs)

**1. Esketamine is FDA-approved for TRD; one acute Phase 3 (TRANSFORM-2) was positive, two were not.** TRANSFORM-2 (flexible dose, adults <65) was positive and a pivotal basis for approval; TRANSFORM-1 (fixed dose) and TRANSFORM-3 (elderly) did not separate from placebo on the primary endpoint. **Confidence: high.**
https://www.psychiatrictimes.com/view/phase-3-transform-trial · https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1064748119305330

**2. Esketamine durability is supported by a randomized-withdrawal design (SUSTAIN-1) and long-term open-label data.** Continuing esketamine after remission/stable response delayed relapse vs placebo (HR ≈ 0.49 for remitters, ≈ 0.30 for responders); ~26.7% relapse on esketamine vs ~45.3% on placebo. SUSTAIN-3 (mean exposure ~42.9 months) showed persistent MADRS improvement and no new safety signals. **Confidence: high.**
https://www.jnj.com/media-center/press-releases/long-term-phase-3-study-shows-esketamine-nasal-spray-plus-an-oral-antidepressant-delayed-time-to-relapse-in-patients-with-treatment-resistant-depression · https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12143125/

**3. Esketamine outperformed quetiapine XR in a head-to-head Phase 3b (ESCAPE-TRD).** A direct active-comparator trial (NEJM 2023) found esketamine superior to quetiapine extended-release for TRD remission and remission maintenance. **Confidence: high.**
https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa2304145

**4. Single-dose racemic ketamine effects are real but short-lived (~1 week); repeated dosing extends benefit.** Meta-analytic data show rapid response after a single dose decaying within roughly a week; repeated administration over 2–3 weeks sustains symptom reduction. **Confidence: high.**
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9010394/ · https://www.thelancet.com/journals/eclinm/article/PIIS2589-5370(23)00304-8/fulltext

**5. Ketamine's antidepressant signal may be substantially expectancy-driven; blinding routinely fails.** Patients and clinicians correctly guess assignment even against active comparators (e.g., midazolam). A trial that masked ketamine under surgical anesthesia (so patients could not perceive the dissociative effect) found no significant antidepressant advantage over placebo, directly implicating expectancy. **Confidence: medium-high** (one well-designed masking trial; needs replication).
https://www.nature.com/articles/s44220-023-00140-x

**6. COMPASS COMP360 psilocybin (Phase 2b, NEJM 2022) met its primary endpoint: 25 mg beat 1 mg by −6.6 MADRS points at week 3 (p<0.001).** The largest psilocybin RCT to date (233 patients, TRD). Week-3 remission ~29% in the 25 mg arm. **Confidence: high** for the short-term primary endpoint.
https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2022/11/03/2547314/0/en/COMPASS-Pathways-announces-publication-of-phase-2b-study-of-COMP360-psilocybin-therapy-for-treatment-resistant-depression-in-The-New-England-Journal-of-Medicine.html · https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36322843/

**7. Psilocybin durability is limited: only ~20% sustained response at week 12 in COMPASS (vs ~10% on 1 mg).** The acute signal attenuates substantially over 12 weeks; most patients are not in durable remission from a single dose. **Confidence: high.**
https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2022/11/03/2547314/0/en/COMPASS-Pathways-announces-publication-of-phase-2b-study-of-COMP360-psilocybin-therapy-for-treatment-resistant-depression-in-The-New-England-Journal-of-Medicine.html

**8. The Carhart-Harris psilocybin-vs-escitalopram trial (NEJM 2021) FAILED its primary endpoint.** QIDS-SR-16 change at week 6: −8.0 (psilocybin) vs −6.0 (escitalopram); between-group difference 2.0 points (95% CI −5.0 to 0.9; p=0.17) — not significant. Some secondary outcomes numerically favored psilocybin, but the trial does **not** establish superiority. Popular "psilocybin beats antidepressants" framing overruns this result. **Confidence: high.**
https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa2032994 · https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37122239/

**9. Psilocybin trials carry serious adverse-event signals including post-session suicidal ideation/behavior.** In COMPASS, suicidal ideation and self-injury occurred across all arms, with several suicidal behaviors among 25 mg non-responders ≥1 month post-dose. **Confidence: medium** (signal real; causality vs underlying TRD unresolved).
https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36322843/

**10. MDMA-assisted therapy showed large Phase 3 PTSD effects (MAPP1 2021, MAPP2 2023).** MAPP1: ~67% no longer met PTSD criteria, ~88% clinically meaningful reduction; CAPS-5 superiority over placebo+therapy (p<0.0001). MAPP2 (Nature Medicine 2023) replicated in a more diverse sample. **Confidence: medium** (large effect sizes, but heavily confounded by unblinding — see below).
https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-023-02565-4 · https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-021-01336-3

**11. FDA declined MDMA-assisted therapy (CRL, Aug 2024); advisory committee voted 2–9 and 1–10 against.** The Psychopharmacologic Drugs Advisory Committee voted 2–9 that efficacy was not demonstrated and 1–10 that benefits did not outweigh risks. FDA requested an additional Phase 3 trial. **Confidence: high.**
https://www.hcplive.com/view/live-updates-fda-psychopharmacologic-advisory-committee-meeting-mdma-ptsd · https://maps.org/2025/09/04/fda-public-release-of-crl/

**12. The MDMA rejection rationale centered on functional unblinding, expectancy bias, inconsistent therapy, and missing safety data.** Documented concerns: near-total functional unblinding (patients/therapists could discern assignment; ~40% had prior MDMA use); no systematic capture of abuse-related adverse events; insufficient durability data; inconsistent/under-documented psychotherapy delivery; patient vulnerability during acute drug effect; and allegations of therapist misconduct and possible data-integrity problems. **Confidence: high.**
https://www.clinicaltrialvanguard.com/opinion/guided-then-penalized-a-deep-dive-into-the-fdas-mdma-ruling-and-what-it-means-for-psychedelic-clinical-trials/ · https://psychedelic.support/resources/fda-decision-lykos-ptsd-mdma-nda-rejected/

**13. The blinding/expectancy confound is the dominant methodological threat across psychedelic RCTs.** Methodologists (Muthukumaraswamy et al. 2021; Aday et al. 2022) argue treatment effect sizes are likely over-estimated because the unmistakable subjective drug effect functionally unblinds participants, and high response expectancy inflates average treatment effects. Proposed remedies include biomarker-driven causal-mediation analysis and novel designs. **Confidence: high** that the confound is serious; **medium** on the magnitude of inflation.
https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/17512433.2021.1933434 · https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC9481484/ · https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S2451902224000557

## Conflicts & uncertainty

- **Is expectancy a "bias" or a "feature"?** MAPS/Lykos and some commentators argue patient expectation is an intrinsic, inseparable component of psychedelic-assisted therapy rather than removable confounding bias. FDA reviewers and independent methodologists treat it as a threat to internal validity that inflates effect sizes. This is a genuine paradigmatic dispute, not a settled fact. (https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12798560/ vs https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/17512433.2021.1933434)
- **Psilocybin's two flagship trials disagree in framing.** COMPASS (vs inactive 1 mg comparator) is positive; Carhart-Harris (vs active escitalopram) is null on its primary endpoint. The comparator choice drives much of the divergence — and neither resolves the unblinding problem.
- **MDMA efficacy vs approvability.** The Phase 3 effect sizes are large and statistically robust on their face; the rejection was about whether those numbers can be trusted given unblinding, therapy heterogeneity, safety gaps, and conduct allegations — not a claim that MDMA is inert.
- **Ketamine durability is comparator- and design-dependent.** The surgical-anesthesia masking trial (Nature Mental Health 2023) is a strong but single challenge to the ketamine literature; it needs replication before treating ketamine's antidepressant effect as largely placebo.
- **Esketamine effect size is modest and partly fragile** (1 of 3 acute pivotals positive); critics note the durability evidence rests on a withdrawal design that can exaggerate maintenance benefit via discontinuation effects. An independent IPD meta-analysis is in progress (medRxiv 2025 preprint).

## What not to absorb yet

- **"Psilocybin is as effective as or better than SSRIs."** The only head-to-head trial (Carhart-Harris 2021) was null on its primary endpoint (p=0.17). Do not encode superiority. (Low/contested.)
- **"A single psychedelic dose produces durable/lasting remission."** COMPASS durability was ~20% sustained response at 12 weeks; not durable for most. (Established that durability is limited.)
- **MDMA Phase 3 headline rates (e.g., 67%/88%) as unbiased efficacy.** These are uncorrected for functional unblinding and expectancy and were judged insufficient by FDA. Flag as confounded, not established. (Medium/contested.)
- **"FDA rejected MDMA because the drug doesn't work."** The rejection was driven by methodological/safety/conduct concerns and unblinding, not a finding of inefficacy. Do not simplify.
- **Ketamine claimed as a robust, blinding-independent antidepressant.** At least one masking trial suggests much of the effect may be expectancy; treat magnitude as uncertain pending replication. (Medium.)
- **Mechanistic "rewiring/neuroplasticity heals depression" claims** for any of these agents — preclinical/mechanistic, not established as the clinical mechanism in humans. (Low; out of scope but commonly overstated in creator framing.)
