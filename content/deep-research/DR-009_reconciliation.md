## Agreements (claims both make — high confidence)

1. **No GLP-1RA is an established/approved addiction treatment.** Both reject "GLP-1 cures addiction"; all human RCTs are phase 2, small, short, and mostly in obese populations.
2. **Alcohol is the strongest human signal.** Both anchor on the Hendershot 2025 *JAMA Psychiatry* semaglutide RCT (n=48, non-treatment-seeking, 9 weeks, low-dose escalation) plus a 2026 *Lancet* RCT in AUD + obesity (n=108).
3. **Hendershot 2025 primary lab endpoint positive.** Both report reduced grams of alcohol consumed in the lab self-administration procedure, β = −0.48, and reduced weekly craving. Both note average drinks per calendar day did NOT significantly fall.
4. **2026 Lancet RCT reduced heavy drinking days** more than placebo in AUD + obesity, framed as first RCT-level evidence in treatment-seekers; both flag obesity-stratified population.
5. **Nicotine craving/consumption dissociation.** The dedicated semaglutide smoking RCT (small, ~24 daily smokers, 9 weeks) reduced nicotine craving and body weight but did NOT significantly reduce cigarettes/day or lab smoking outcomes. Both treat this as the key caution: craving ≠ cessation.
6. **Smoking cessation efficacy is NOT established.** Both reject "GLP-1s help people quit smoking."
7. **Observational signals are large (~50%) but non-causal.** Both cite Wang/Volkow Nature Communications EHR cohort (~50% lower AUD incidence/recurrence) and warn of healthy-adherer/indication/surveillance bias; both say "association, not efficacy."
8. **Mechanism: preclinical case is robust, human bridge incomplete.** Both agree GLP-1Rs are in mesolimbic reward circuitry (VTA, NAc) with NTS/brainstem relays, that rodent data show blunted drug/food-evoked dopamine and reduced seeking, but that direct human mesolimbic target engagement is unproven.
9. **"Anti-reward vs general consummatory brake" is unresolved.** Both raise that effects may be mediated by appetite/satiety/weight loss/behavioral support rather than addiction-specific neurobiology, made hard to disentangle by obesity-restricted samples.
10. **Durability untested; weight-regain analogy argues caution.** Both note documented post-cessation weight regain and that no addiction trial has post-discontinuation craving/drinking follow-up; durability should be assumed unknown.
11. **Tirzepatide human addiction evidence is preliminary.** Both: mainly observational/self-report + a 2023 obesity self-report study (Sci Reports) and ongoing/registered phase 2 trials; no completed addiction RCT.
12. **Food/appetite signal is real (medium-high); BED RCT evidence weak.** Both agree semaglutide reduces food cravings/energy-dense food preference in obesity, but rigorous BED RCTs are largely absent; behavioral (non-ingestive) addictions are speculative.
13. **Safety: GI effects dominate; addiction-specific safety in lean/malnourished/severe-SUD populations is under-studied.**

## Verdict

Convergence is high on the qualitative skeleton: alcohol is the strongest human signal (two small/moderate phase-2 RCTs plus large but confounded observational cohorts), the nicotine craving-vs-consumption dissociation is a real and important caution, mechanism is robust in rodents but unproven for human mesolimbic engagement, observational ~50% effects are association-not-causation, durability is untested, and tirzepatide/behavioral addictions remain speculative. These shared claims now warrant **higher confidence** because two independent pipelines reached them with overlapping primary sources (Hendershot 2025, the 2026 Lancet RCT, Wang/Volkow, the semaglutide smoking RCT). The reports do **not** truly contradict each other on facts; they differ mainly in coverage depth and in two areas warranting **lower/held confidence pending paper-level verification**: (1) the exact Hendershot secondary-endpoint profile — the agent's broader positive read (peak BAC and drinks-per-drinking-day both significant) should be confirmed against the primary article, since CDR's more conservative quantity/craving framing and its dose nuance (lab effect at 0.5 mg) are not reconciled; and (2) the 2026 Lancet effect size — the agent's "~12 vs ~8 days / ~50% greater" figure is exactly the number CDR explicitly refused to assert without direct extraction, so it should be treated as provisional. CDR is the more skeptical and source-disciplined report (richer null-trial coverage: exenatide AUD, dulaglutide smoking, liraglutide BED; explicit verification checklist), while the agent adds useful specifics (Wang/Volkow correction, opioid/cannabis cohorts, weight-regain meta-regression) but occasionally states effect sizes more confidently than the underlying verification supports.
