## Agreements (high-confidence — both reports independently assert)

1. **The gut-brain axis is biologically real** as a set of bidirectional signaling channels (vagal, SCFA, serotonin/enterochromaffin, immune/inflammatory), but the evidence is sharply asymmetric: mechanism is established in rodents (germ-free/gnotobiotic), human causal demonstration is thin and largely correlational.

2. **Vagal pathway is strong in rodents, indirect/unproven in humans.** Both state there is no human RCT demonstrating a vagally mediated probiotic mood effect; human data are at best compatible with vagal involvement.

3. **The "90–95% of serotonin is in the gut → gut controls brain serotonin/mood" claim is a category error.** Both affirm the biochemistry (~90–95% body serotonin from enterochromaffin cells) but stress that peripheral serotonin does NOT cross the blood-brain barrier; any CNS effect must be indirect (tryptophan/kynurenine, vagal, immune).

4. **SCFAs (butyrate, propionate, acetate) are a plausible gut-to-brain signal — mechanistically supported preclinically, human mood causality unproven.** Both rate this medium preclinical / low human.

5. **Rodent FMT phenotype-transfer (depressed-human or stressed-rodent microbiota → recipient depression-like behavior) is the strongest mechanistic causal claim but does not establish human causation.** Both flag the ~95% phenotype-transfer rate in human-microbiota-associated rodent studies as implausibly high, signaling over-interpretation / publication bias.

6. **Germ-free / gnotobiotic models do not translate cleanly to humans** (abnormal immune development, microbial exposure, baseline physiology). High confidence in both.

7. **SMILES (Jacka 2017) is a landmark but methodologically fragile single/assessor-blind diet RCT;** the published Molendijk critique argues expectancy / loss-of-blinding / recruitment likely inflated the large effect. Neither trial established a microbiome mechanism. Both treat the *direction* (diet quality helps) as more credible than the *magnitude*.

8. **HELFIMED is a directional replication but not clean** (bundles diet, fish oil, food provision, social/group contact); does not isolate a microbiome pathway.

9. **Diet improvement for depression is the single most defensible human-facing claim** (medium confidence, replicated, magnitude uncertain due to unavoidable lack of participant blinding).

10. **Probiotic meta-analytic effects are statistically significant but fragile and heterogeneous;** stand-alone/general-population probiotic use is weak-to-null while clinically diagnosed / adjunctive use looks better. Both cite the same large clinical-sample estimates (depression SMD ≈ −0.96, anxiety SMD ≈ −0.59) and both note smaller estimates elsewhere (≈ −0.34).

11. **The same "do not absorb yet" list:** probiotics as a proven depression treatment; the serotonin syllogism; leaky-gut-causes-depression as established human causation; strain-specific psychobiotic mood-drug claims; rodent phenotype transfer as proof of human causation; SMILES's effect *size* as reliable.

## Verdict

Convergence is strong on the qualitative core: both reports independently reach the same skeptical thesis — the gut-brain axis is real, mechanism is rodent-established, human causal evidence is thin/correlational, diet-quality-for-depression is the most defensible human claim (direction credible, magnitude inflated by unblindable trials), probiotics are oversold, and the same five-or-six popular claims (serotonin syllogism, leaky-gut causation, strain-specific mood drugs, phenotype-transfer-as-human-proof, SMILES effect size) should NOT be absorbed. Because they were generated independently and agree on all of this, those qualitative judgments warrant **higher** confidence. They also independently converge on the headline clinical-sample meta-analytic numbers (depression SMD ≈ −0.96, anxiety ≈ −0.59, and smaller ≈ −0.34 elsewhere), so those point estimates are reasonably solid — **but their fragility is the load-bearing finding, not the magnitude.** The two reports are largely **complementary rather than contradictory**: there are few genuine factual conflicts, mostly differing emphasis and source selection (CDR is paper-level and quantitative on mechanism/single trials — Bravo/Kelly, Dalile, Nikolova 2021's adjunctive split, Pinto-Sanchez; the agent is stronger on bias-correction statistics, GRADE, leaky-gut framing, and durability). The main item warranting **lower / hold confidence** is the agent's specific I²/shrinkage/GRADE figures: the CDR explicitly could not verify these from abstracts and flagged them for local checking, so they should be confirmed against the primary meta-analyses (PMC12166186 / Nutrition Reviews 83(7) / Asad 2025) before being hard-coded. Likewise the adjunctive-vs-standalone contrast (CDR-only) and the Dalile SCFA effect metrics should be verified from primaries. No fabricated agreement: where one report carries a claim the other is silent on (Kelly 2017, AMMEND, human FMT, Annals 2025, leaky-gut/LPS), it is listed as unique, not shared.
