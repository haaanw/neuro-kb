# Source Note — DR-004: Melatonin: physiologic phase-shifter vs supratherapeutic hypnotic

## Verdict

Melatonin has two distinct pharmacologies that popular framing conflates. As a chronobiotic, low physiologic doses (~0.3-0.5 mg) timed relative to DLMO reset the SCN clock via MT1/MT2 receptors; timing matters far more than dose, and 0.5 mg equals 3.0 mg in phase-shifting power when each is optimally timed (Burgess 2010, HIGH). As a hypnotic, larger OTC doses (3-10 mg) yield only a small effect (~7 min faster sleep onset, ~8 min more sleep; Ferracioli-Oda 2013, HIGH) that is widely overstated. By condition: jet lag is best supported (Cochrane: effective for >=5 eastward zones; 0.5-5 mg similar, >5 mg no better) and non-24 in blind adults is the one indication with an approved agonist (tasimelteon); DSWPD is supported only by a weak AASM recommendation on low-quality evidence; shift work disorder is weakest. Two real safety/quality problems: commercial label inaccuracy (-83% to +478% of label) with serotonin contamination in ~1/4 of products (Erland & Saxena 2017), and a 530% rise in pediatric ingestions with hospitalizations and two deaths (CDC MMWR 2022). The adolescent HPG/puberty concern is biologically plausible (kisspeptin-GnRH interaction) but remains LOW-confidence: long-term human data are reassuring-but-thin with no adequately powered RCT. Creator claims that low-dose melatonin is a reliable "reset button," that more is better for sleep, or that it is risk-free for kids all outrun the evidence.
