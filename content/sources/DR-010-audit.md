# Source Note — DR-010: Gut-brain axis & psychobiotics: human evidence vs mouse hype

- **type:** autonomous web deep-research (agent), primary-source cited

- **report:** [[DR-010]]

## Verdict

The microbiota-gut-brain axis is mechanistically established largely in germ-free/gnotobiotic rodents, while human causal evidence is thin and mostly correlational. Of the four signaling pathways: vagal and SCFA signaling are strong in mice but only indirectly inferred in humans; the "90-95% of serotonin is in the gut" fact is real biochemistry but peripheral serotonin does NOT cross the blood-brain barrier, so the popular leap to "probiotics boost brain serotonin" is a category error; the immune/inflammation (leaky gut/LPS) link to depression is robustly associated in humans but its gut-causation arm rests on rodent FMT studies. The flagship preclinical paradigm (transplanting depressed-human microbiota into germ-free mice and reproducing depression-like behavior) replicates in animals, but a Cell analysis found ~95% of human-microbiota-associated rodent studies report phenotype transfer, an implausibly high rate signaling over-claiming. On interventions: dietary RCTs (SMILES, HELFIMED, AMMEND) report large depression reductions with partial replication, but all share fatal blinding limits (you cannot blind diet), small samples, and subjective outcomes; SMILES drew a published critique that undisclosed recruitment and imperfect blinding likely inflated its effect. Probiotic meta-analyses show significant but fragile effects (depression SMD ~ -0.34 to -0.96 across reviews) that shrink ~30% when high-risk trials are removed, with high heterogeneity (I2 ~80-85%), substantial publication bias, and low/moderate GRADE certainty. The best-blinded probiotic trials (e.g., Nikolova 2023 JAMA Psychiatry) are explicitly pilots. Defensible human claim: diet-quality improvement plausibly helps depression (medium confidence, direction more credible than magnitude). Overstated: probiotics as a mood treatment/cure.
