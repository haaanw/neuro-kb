## Verdict

Esketamine (Spravato) has the firmest evidence: FDA-approved 2019 for treatment-resistant depression on one positive acute Phase 3 (TRANSFORM-2), a positive randomized-withdrawal relapse-prevention trial (SUSTAIN-1), long-term open-label data (SUSTAIN-3), and later superiority over quetiapine XR (ESCAPE-TRD, NEJM 2023) — but two of three acute pivotals (TRANSFORM-1, TRANSFORM-3 elderly) were negative, so the effect is real but modest. Racemic ketamine gives rapid but short-lived benefit (~1 week single-dose) and its effect may be heavily expectancy-driven (a surgical-anesthesia masking trial found no advantage over placebo). Psilocybin: COMPASS COMP360 Phase 2b (NEJM 2022) met its primary endpoint (25 mg beat 1 mg by -6.6 MADRS at week 3, p<0.001) but durability was weak (~20% sustained response at week 12), and the head-to-head Carhart-Harris trial vs escitalopram (NEJM 2021) FAILED its primary endpoint (QIDS-SR-16 difference 2.0 points, p=0.17). MDMA-assisted therapy showed large Phase 3 PTSD effects (MAPP1/MAPP2) but FDA declined approval Aug 2024; the advisory committee voted 2-9 (efficacy) and 1-10 (benefit/risk) against, citing severe functional unblinding, expectancy bias, inconsistent/unverifiable therapy delivery, missing abuse-potential and cardiovascular safety data, durability gaps, and therapist-misconduct/data-integrity allegations; FDA requested a new Phase 3. The dominant cross-cutting confound is functional unblinding/expectancy: the unmistakable subjective drug effect lets patients and raters guess assignment, likely inflating effect sizes — established as a serious threat, though whether it is removable "bias" or an intrinsic "feature" of the therapy is genuinely contested.
