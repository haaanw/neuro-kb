## Verdict

Moncrieff et al. 2022 (Molecular Psychiatry umbrella review) found no consistent evidence that depression is caused by low serotonin, refuting the public "chemical-imbalance" model. Crucially, this does NOT refute SSRI efficacy or mechanism — causation of disease and treatment efficacy are separate questions, a point made forcefully by ~30+ pharmacologists who called the review a strawman attack. On efficacy: Cipriani 2018 (522 RCTs, ~116k patients) shows all antidepressants beat placebo, but the effect is modest (SMD ~0.30, response ORs ~1.6-1.7, NNT ~7-9); Fournier 2010 shows benefit is severity-dependent, minimal for mild-moderate depression and clinically meaningful mainly at severe baseline (HDRS ~25+). The 2-4 week delayed onset (serotonin rises within hours) is the main argument for downstream-neuroplasticity mechanisms — BDNF, cAMP-CREB, hippocampal neurogenesis, synaptogenesis — now supported by a human PET RCT showing escitalopram raises synaptic density over 3-5 weeks; well-supported as a phenomenon but causal chain in humans still inferential. On harms: best placebo-adjusted withdrawal incidence is ~15% (1 in 6-7), severe ~1 in 35 (Henssler 2024), versus the non-placebo-adjusted ~56%/46%-severe Davies-Read 2019 figure that overstates it. Treatment-emergent sexual dysfunction is genuinely common (~40-65%+ with structured assessment, Montejo). PSSD is EMA-recognized (2019) as real but its incidence is essentially unquantified. Emotional blunting affects ~40-60% of treated patients (Goodwin/Price OQuESA survey).
