# Source Note — DR-011: Predictive processing & free-energy: empirical support vs overreach

## Verdict

Predictive processing must be unbundled into claims of decreasing empirical strength. (1) That the cortex is predictive/anticipatory is uncontested and replicated (mismatch, expectation, surprise signals) — HIGH. (2) That it implements predictive coding specifically (dedicated error units, hierarchical generative model, feedback=predictions / feedforward=errors) is MEDIUM: newly supported by human 7T laminar fMRI in V1 (errors superficial, predictions deep; Aitken 2024) and mouse L2/3 mismatch neurons (Keller & Mrsic-Flogel 2018), but the canonical microcircuit (Bastos 2012) is still a theoretical mapping and many 'error' signals are confounded by adaptation, novelty, surprise, and attention. (3) Precision-weighting mapped onto ACh (expected/sensory precision via gain) and NE/LC (volatility/unexpected uncertainty), with attention as precision optimization, is MEDIUM-to-LOW: coherent and supported by suggestive rat pharmacology (Perez-Gonzalez 2024) and pupillometry (Nassar 2012), but causal receptor- and layer-specific human evidence is thin and the pupil-LC proxy is unreliable (Megemont 2022). (4) The Free Energy Principle as a grand unifying theory of all biological/self-organizing systems is, by Friston's own framing, a near-tautological mathematical principle (like least action), not a falsifiable hypothesis; philosophers argue its generality is tautology not unification — LOW as an empirical claim. The defensible line: predictive coding is a productive, partially-confirmed process theory; the FEP's falsifiable scientific content just IS those process theories, not the principle. 'Brain minimizes free energy therefore X' is heuristic, not evidence, and is where creator/popular framing most outruns the data.
