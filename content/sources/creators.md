# Comparable Creators (credibility-graded)

> Credibility tiers flagged. Primary scientist > science communicator > commercial/low-rigor.
> Always cross-check claims against primary literature regardless of tier.

| Creator | Platform | Handle/URL | Focus | Credibility |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Andrew Huberman | YouTube + podcast | https://www.youtube.com/@hubermanlab (Huberman Lab) | Neuroscience-based health, behavior, and performance protocols (sleep, dopamine, focus, hormones) | Primary scientist (tenured Stanford neurobiology professor); credible credentials, though some peers criticize occasional overstatement/oversimplification of protocols and supplement promotion. |
| Peter Attia | Podcast + YouTube | https://peterattiamd.com/podcast / @PeterAttiaMD | Longevity, metabolic health, cardiovascular disease, exercise, and lifespan/healthspan medicine | Primary/clinical (Stanford-trained physician, MD); rigorous and evidence-focused, one of the most credible voices in the longevity space. |
| Rhonda Patrick | Podcast + YouTube | https://www.foundmyfitness.com / @FoundMyFitness | Nutrition, micronutrients, aging biology, exercise, and cellular health | Science communicator with real credentials (PhD in biomedical science); generally rigorous and citation-heavy, though sells a paid membership and supplements. |
| Robert Sapolsky | YouTube (Stanford lectures, interviews) | Stanford channel lectures + guest appearances; no personal show | Neuroendocrinology, stress biology, primate behavior, and the biology of human behavior/free will | Primary scientist (Stanford professor, MacArthur Fellow); highly credible academic authority, not a commercial creator. |
| Lex Fridman | Podcast + YouTube | https://lexfridman.com / @lexfridman | Long-form interviews spanning AI, neuroscience, and the mind (hosts neuroscientists like Sapolsky, Walker, Karl Friston) | Science communicator/interviewer (MIT-affiliated research background); credible host who platforms real scientists, but topics range very broadly beyond neuroscience and guest vetting is uneven. |
| Matthew Walker | Podcast + YouTube | https://www.youtube.com/@mattwalkerpodcast (The Matt Walker Podcast) | Sleep science, circadian rhythms, dreaming, and the effect of sleep on brain/body | Primary scientist (UC Berkeley professor of neuroscience); credible authority, though his book 'Why We Sleep' drew some criticism for data overstatement. |
| David Eagleman | Podcast + YouTube | https://eagleman.com/podcast (Inner Cosmos with David Eagleman) | Perception, time, sensory substitution, neuroplasticity, and how the brain constructs reality | Primary scientist (Stanford adjunct professor, PBS 'The Brain' presenter); credible neuroscientist and strong communicator. |
| Lisa Feldman Barrett | YouTube talks + books/podcast guest | https://lisafeldmanbarrett.com | Affective neuroscience, the constructed theory of emotion, interoception, and brain prediction | Primary scientist (University Distinguished Professor at Northeastern); top-cited researcher, highly credible. |
| Anil Seth | YouTube / TED + books | https://www.anilseth.com / @anilkseth | Consciousness, the predictive brain, and the neuroscience of perception/self | Primary scientist (Professor of Cognitive and Computational Neuroscience, University of Sussex); rigorous academic communicator. |
| Wendy Suzuki | YouTube / TED + books + podcast guest | https://www.wendysuzuki.com / @wendy.suzuki | Exercise and the brain, memory, attention, and the neuroscience of anxiety | Primary scientist (NYU professor of neural science, Dean); credible researcher, leans toward accessible/popular framing. |
| Sam Harris | Podcast + app | https://www.samharris.org/podcast (Making Sense) / @SamHarrisOrg | Mind, consciousness, meditation, free will, and philosophy/neuroscience intersections | Science communicator with a PhD in cognitive neuroscience (UCLA); credible and intellectually rigorous, but content is more philosophical/opinion-driven than primary research. |
| Scott Barry Kaufman | Podcast | https://scottbarrykaufman.com/podcast (The Psychology Podcast) | Cognitive science, intelligence, creativity, well-being, and human potential | Primary scientist/communicator (PhD cognitive psychology, Yale; taught at Columbia/Penn); credible, citation-driven interviews (flagship show recently concluded with a large archive). |
| Steven Pinker | YouTube talks + books + podcast guest | https://stevenpinker.com / @sapinker | Cognitive psychology, language, the mind, and reasoning | Primary scientist (Harvard psychology professor); credible academic authority, no dedicated show but a frequent expert guest. |
| Russell Barkley | YouTube | https://www.youtube.com/@russellbarkleyphd | ADHD science, executive function, and clinical neuropsychology | Primary/clinical (retired clinical professor of psychiatry); widely regarded as a leading evidence-based ADHD authority. |
| Tim Ferriss | Podcast + YouTube | https://tim.blog/podcast (The Tim Ferriss Show) / @timferriss | Performance, habits, health optimization; hosts scientists and physicians as guests | Science communicator/interviewer, NOT a scientist; credible platform that surfaces real experts, but content is self-experimentation and lifestyle-oriented rather than primary science. |
| Gabor Mate | YouTube + books + podcast guest | https://drgabormate.com | Trauma, addiction, stress, and mind-body health | MIXED credibility: a licensed physician with influential ideas, but several of his trauma/disease causation claims are not well-supported by mainstream evidence; treat as opinion, not settled science. |
| Bryan Johnson | YouTube + podcast | https://www.youtube.com/@bryanjohnson (Blueprint) | Anti-aging self-experimentation, biomarkers, and longevity protocols | COMMERCIAL / low-rigor: tech entrepreneur, not a scientist; n=1 self-experimentation tied to a Blueprint product line — engaging but should not be taken as generalizable science. |
| Mark Hyman | Podcast + YouTube | https://drhyman.com (The Doctor's Farmacy) / @drmarkhyman | Functional medicine, nutrition, and metabolic/'food as medicine' health | MIXED/COMMERCIAL: a credentialed MD, but 'functional medicine' framing and supplement/product promotion draw frequent criticism for outrunning the evidence — verify claims independently. |
| Dave Asprey | Podcast + YouTube | https://daveasprey.com (The Human Upgrade / formerly Bulletproof Radio) | 'Biohacking,' supplements, diet, and longevity hacks | LOW credibility / commercial: entrepreneur with no relevant scientific degree; heavily product-driven (Bulletproof brand) and routinely makes overstated, poorly-evidenced claims. |
| Joe Dispenza | YouTube + podcast + workshops | https://drjoedispenza.com / @drjoedispenza | 'Rewiring the brain,' meditation, manifestation, and self-healing | LOW credibility / pseudoscience: chiropractor (not a neuroscientist) who promotes scientifically unsupported healing and 'manifestation' claims tied to paid programs — flag as unreliable. |

## Low-credibility — use with caution / flag in notes

- **Robert Sapolsky** — Primary scientist (Stanford professor, MacArthur Fellow); highly credible academic authority, not a commercial creator.
- **Gabor Mate** — MIXED credibility: a licensed physician with influential ideas, but several of his trauma/disease causation claims are not well-supported by mainstream evidence; treat as opinion, not settled science.
- **Bryan Johnson** — COMMERCIAL / low-rigor: tech entrepreneur, not a scientist; n=1 self-experimentation tied to a Blueprint product line — engaging but should not be taken as generalizable science.
- **Mark Hyman** — MIXED/COMMERCIAL: a credentialed MD, but 'functional medicine' framing and supplement/product promotion draw frequent criticism for outrunning the evidence — verify claims independently.
- **Dave Asprey** — LOW credibility / commercial: entrepreneur with no relevant scientific degree; heavily product-driven (Bulletproof brand) and routinely makes overstated, poorly-evidenced claims.
- **Joe Dispenza** — LOW credibility / pseudoscience: chiropractor (not a neuroscientist) who promotes scientifically unsupported healing and 'manifestation' claims tied to paid programs — flag as unreliable.
