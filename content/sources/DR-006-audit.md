# Source Note — DR-006: Testosterone boosters & TRT cardiovascular/fertility safety

## Verdict

The best RCT-grade lifestyle lever on endogenous testosterone is weight loss in overweight/obese men: ~10% diet-induced loss raises total T ~2-3 nmol/L (~85 ng/dL); bariatric surgery (~30% loss) up to ~8.7 nmol/L (~250 ng/dL). Sleep restriction may lower T ~10-15% (one crossover) but a later RCT found no change — contested. Exercise has negligible effect on resting T in meta-analysis. Among supplements, marketing outruns evidence: ashwagandha has the most RCT support (~+90-120 ng/dL in small, often industry-funded trials) but a real, RUCAM-confirmed hepatotoxicity signal (one transplant; 2024 TGA alert). Tongkat Ali shows modest benefit in older/low-T men. Zinc helps mainly by correcting deficiency. Boron is thin/inconsistent in men. Fadogia agrestis has zero human trials and rodent testicular-toxicity data — pure creator/podcast hype. On TRT: TRAVERSE (NEJM 2023, n=5,246 high-CV-risk hypogonadal men, transdermal gel) showed MACE noninferiority (HR 0.96, 95% CI 0.78-1.17) but higher atrial fibrillation, pulmonary embolism, and acute kidney injury. TRT predictably suppresses spermatogenesis (contraceptive effect, mostly reversible 6-12 months; hCG can preserve fertility) and raises hematocrit (keep <54%).
